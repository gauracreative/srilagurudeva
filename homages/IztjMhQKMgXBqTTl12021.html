Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Candramukhi dasi

Australia, Murwillumbah

Dear Srila Gurudeva,

I offer my heartfelt pranamas at your lotus feet and also unto all my gurus and guardians in our Gaudiya sampradaya, unto Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu and all His associates, and unto Sri Sri Radha Krsna Yugala-Kisora, accompanied by Lalita and Visakha and all Their associates.

I am sitting at your feet, at this moment knowing you are always with me, that your hand is always over my head and that you will never leave me. Too often I forget this fact and become absorbed this way or that in maya’s dancing plays and believing what my eyes perceive - that you have disappeared from our vision. But really you have never left. You are in my heart and everywhere around me as my guide and friend. The world is your asrama, with so many lessons coming from you, my teacher, at every moment.

At this time of your hundredth appearance, ten years after your disappearance, I reflect and look back to those years when you were physically present and how inspired I was, I think about how these last ten years have passed with many distractions, and I look towards the rest of my life and pray that I may be engaged only in your seva.

I’m so grateful that you came into my life at the young age of seventeen and that you guided me through my very formative young years into your service. Meeting you felt like coming home, to my heart-home, where no further searching for anything else was required. Bathing in your love and affection brought many tears to my eyes. You made Krsna consciousness become alive for me and inspired me so deeply. That first time you arrived at our home you gazed deep into my soul with your blue lotus eyes.

“I will help you”, you said, as I placed a tuba-rose garland around your neck. You came in dreams to me every night that first year (and from time to time over the years) and then two weeks after meeting you, you accepted me as your darling daughter. My mother, Mekhala Dasi, asked for initiation on behalf of my sister Ragalekha and I because we were crying so much we couldn’t speak.

“Don’t worry, I have accepted you,” you said, cupping your hands. “I am taking your tears and offering them unto the lotus feet of Sri Sri Radha and Krsna.”

My heart became yours forever.

My journey home had really begun because now I was connected to a near and dear servant of Srimati Radhika. You brought me to Vraja Mandala parikrama nearly every year for fourteen years and visited us in Australia ten times, four of which were at my family home, which you affectionately called your home in Glenock Road, Uki. You injected your powerful harikatha into my heart and directed me to study your words and preach. By your extraordinary power you had me, a young girl in her twenties travel in Australia, New Zealand and then the world speaking and sharing your words on your behalf! You were the perfect flute player, otherwise, how could I have done that? You encouraged us to become like a flute which is hollow and not blocked by any false ego or ulterior motives, so you can play us. You entered me into the school of how to be like your flute, gradually clearing away any blockages. You told my sister Ragalekha and I to preach and become like lions to cross over all apa-siddhanta, philosophical conclusions that are opposed to the teachings of our guru-parampara.

Attracting us with your sweet harikatha and your perfect kindness and love, you invited us into your family, which is Sri Gauranga and Sri Sri Radha and Krishna’s family. You spoke of Sri Krsna’s intimate loving exchanges with His associates and how they all regard Him as their intimate family member, forgetting His majesty of being God (lokik-sadabandhuvat), while at the same time exhibiting that Vraja mood yourself by being so open, loving and approachable like our family member. You spoke in detail about pure bhakti and the importance of fixing our goal in life, establishing in my heart a desire to at least have the desire for the desire to attain the loving service of Sri Radha as Her maidservant. You described the gradations of bhakti, as outlined in Srimad Bhagavatam, including the moods of Dhruva Maharaja, Prahlad Maharaja, Ambarish Maharaja, Hanuman, Uddhava and the residents of Dvaraka, finally arriving in Vrndavana with the sweet moods of the Vrajavasis, headed by Srimati Radharani and the gopis, attracting us towards the topmost goal. You said that you want all of this tattva and siddhanta to manifest in our hearts, which surely will happen if we follow your instructions.

Your harikatha was like a nectar tonic for the ears and the heart (hrt-karana- rasayanah katha), cleansing away my doubts and ignorance and bringing me close to your lotus feet, inspiring my inner urge for loving Sri Sri Radha and Krsna to awaken just a little bit. Although I may have been in a crowd of a hundred or a thousand people, mystically I always felt like you were speaking directly to me. My godbrothers and godsisters expressed the same thing, for you are like Sukadeva Gosvami, who can enter the hearts of all. Your instructions contain so much power; they instilled in us the ability to follow them if we simply tried. Once establishing our goal, you outlined how we can attain this pinnacle of devotion from the lowest point, beginning from transcendental faith (sraddha), all the way up to the first sprout of krsna-prema (bhava), and then to the delight of the highest stages of love of God (prema).

“Don’t be weak, be strong”, you said, and at other times, “Don’t be hopeless”. One time I asked you how I can be strong, and you replied,

“Before you go to do anything, always think, would my Gurudeva do that?”

You outlined how guru-nistha is the backbone of bhakti. Without a backbone one cannot walk, so without guru-nistha one cannot go forward in bhakti. To build that faith, you expressed,

“I am always with you. You should think that my hand is always over your head, I am closer to you than your life breath,” and you confirmed this in many ways, showing that you knew my heart.

One time I said to you, “I think you have come to me in some dreams.”

You sweetly replied, “Thank you.”

You kindly engaged my sister Ragalekha and I, along with many other girls, in singing beautiful bhajanas and astakams for you, teaching us how to become absorbed and offer our heart through such singing. You engaged us in performing many dramas of Krsna’s sweet pastimes and other lilas of the Lord and His devotees, instructing us that the most important thing when performing a drama is to make strong impressions in the heart of the audience. It was always so wonderful to receive a pat on the head after we had pleased you in this way.

Since your physical departure from our presence, my life has taken various directions due to my own material desires and inability to completely surrender. However, your grace and guidance has been ever present, leading me to deeper and stronger realisations of those things you spoke about and absorbed me in, through hard knocks and the tumbles and turns of life experience. I am unlimitedly grateful that God was so merciful to send you to me, and that you are my eternal master.

As the eastern horizon becomes tinged with soft rays of saffron, heralding the rise of the sun, you manifested in my life shining the light to indicate that the darkness of my sojourn in this material world will soon be over. I pray that I may always follow your instruction to not look left or right, but to be one pointed and engage my body, mind and words in your service, so that I will again be physically with you, but this time in my eternal spiritual form under your guidance at the lotus feet of Srimati Radhika.

Your insignificant daughter,

Candramukhi Devi Dasi
