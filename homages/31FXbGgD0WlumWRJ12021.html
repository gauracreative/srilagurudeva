Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Vishwambhara dasa

United States, San Francisco

Wedding of Vishwambhara Das and Gauravani D Dasi May, 01, 2009

I offer my humble obeisances at the lotus feet of my beloved Gurudeva, Sri Srimad Bhaktivedanta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja.

My Dearest Bhakta-Bandhava Srila Gurudeva!

All glories to your Divine Grace who is blessing my existence and nurturing my quest into golden divinity.

You have delivered not just previous generations of my family but future generations of all families. Your meetings with Acarya Kesari and then his brother, Srila Prabhupada, set in motion the golden age. Your hands made the ceremonious arrangement to decorate Srila Prabhupada with anuraga and assisted Acarya Kesari in saving Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Saraswati Thakura’s movement.

Prior to arriving to the birth place of my father and forefathers, in the west, Srila Prabhupada left in your care his golden Mahaprabhu of Jhansi. This worship guaranteed success for all.

Your solitary Govardhana bhajana gave favour and protection to Srila Prabhupada in his “lonely years” at New York, as my parents were to be discovered and saved by him.

You manifested, from the spiritual sky, the first set of deities in the West – Sri Sri Radha Govinda, displaying the Supreme God-Personality and in Their Colourful Forms of worship for my family in the West to decorate, celebrate, and worship – singing “Govindam-Adi-Purusam”.

You equipped Srila Prabhupada with the weapons of Mahaprabhu: books, mrdanga and kartalas utilized by my family to combat Kali-yuga as they were being jailed for street sankirtana from Costa Rica to San Francisco, starting first centres globally.

Your service unto Narottama das Thakura’s sacred Bengal pracara along with the Navadvipa parikramas was a key cornerstone of the golden era expanding in India. The sambhoga you assembled between Vraja and Bengal, setting the groundwork for my family to arrive and be welcomed with open arms is a miracle.

In the late 1970’s, when Srila Prabhupada was holding your hand in a very low, soft, and choked voice said to you, “After my departure, you must take care so that what I have done will go on well….I have one special request-that you will put me in samadhi with your own hand….” Later my father would discover this truth, hidden in BBT tapes covered in Bengali.

During Srila Prabhupada’s centennial year, on June 28, 1996 in Badger, CA, my families of many fallen sannyasis, gurus, fathers and mothers, teenagers who all represented the hurt, disabled, weakened, exhausted West got a glimpse of reignited bliss with your appearance. We were all finished, worn, done, from the war we waged on maya, for decades in separation from our Founder Acarya. We were the patriotic veterans of the golden-era, so we ran, in hiding, for protection to the hills of Badger, to the villages of Europe, to towns outside the sprawling cities of Berkeley to grow with our families, remembering and worshiping “your ever well-wisher,” your brother, forgetting what daylight looked like.

You told us all, “He ordered me. I came to help. To help only. And to take the foot dust of Swamiji wherever he went….I want to strengthen ISKCON. You are all my gurus, my siksa-gurus. I am glad you are helping me. This is his greatness. I am so glad to be among you...like istagosti. I am not giving class. Questions and answers. I am not thinking I am guru and acarya. I am thinking you are disciples of Swamji and we are all brothers.”

Sitting upon your bed, you gave us friendship in intimacy and simplicity. You gave us hugs, you kissed our souls, and forever captured our hearts. You gave me My Ever Well Wisher.

You were kind to us all, we were the neediest of them all, as we knew what gold tasted like, but lost it and didn’t even know we were dying of thirst.

anuvratānāṁ śiṣyāṇāṁ / putrāṇāṁ ca dvijottama
anāpṛṣṭam api brūyur / guravo dīna-vatsalāḥ
–Śrimad Bhagavatam 3.7.36

“O best among the brāhmaṇas, those who are spiritual masters are very kind to the needy. They are always kind to their followers, disciples and sons, and without being asked by them, the spiritual master describes all that is knowledge.”

You travelled the world more than 30 times, and established over 100,000 temples in the hearts of your followers. You printed and distributed millions of books and sponsored the printing of Srila Prabhupada's original books, expanding his teachings and deepening the appreciation of his books, including our entire guru-varga. Nobody else has done this in our history.

etān me pṛcchataḥ praśnān / hareḥ karma-vivitsayā
brūhi me ’jñasya mitratvād / ajayā naṣṭa-cakṣuṣaḥ
–Śrimad Bhagavatam 3.7.40

“My dear sage, I have put all these questions before you with a view to knowing the pastimes of Hari, the Supreme Personality of Godhead. You are the friend of all, so kindly describe them for all those who have lost their vision.”

You are the friend of all, you are Bhakta-Bandhav.

sarve vedāś ca yajñāś ca / tapo dānāni cānagha
jīvābhaya-pradānasya na / kurvīran kalām api
–Śrimad Bhagavatam 3.7.41

“O spotless one, your answers to all these questions will grant immunity from all material miseries. Such charity is greater than all Vedic charities, sacrifices, penances, etc.”

You are the greatest charity to the needy. Those that gave their lives to the movement, you cared for them, in your old age, and united them with your Vraja-vasi, Hindustani, and Bengali devotees to create one united family, in the name of the Bhaktivedantas.

Wealth is rare and real wealth is even more rare. Only the few are able to get gold, but many benefit from it. And so alive are your veterans, patriots of the Golden Movement, and with your friendship they will expand your charity, in your name, always.

Your faithful and loyal son,

Vishwambhara dasa (San Francisco, CA)
