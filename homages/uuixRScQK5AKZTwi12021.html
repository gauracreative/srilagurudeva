Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Rohininandan Das

India, Vrindavan

nama om visnu-padaya radhikayai-priyatmane
sri-srimad bhaktvedanta-narayana iti namine

I offer my humble obeisances at the lotus feet of my beloved Gurudeva, Sri Srimad Bhaktivedanta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja.

On the most blessed occasion of Srila Gurudeva’s centennial Vyasa-puja celebration, I take this opportunity to remember some of my golden moments with him which I cherish every day. I have two thoughts––one is his physical separation of ten years, but the second thought is remembering his ever-fresh eternal moods, which are the source of our daily sustenance on the path of bhakti.

Srila Gurudeva broadened our conception about guru-disciple relationship by introducing the concept of bhagavat-guru-parampara and clarified its speciality over pancaratrika-guru-parampara. In pancaratrika-guru-parampara, guru gives initiation to disciple just by telling him the mantra only, without having any realization of the mantra and by performing the ritualistic fire yajna. Bhagavat-guru-parampara, on the other hand, necessarily includes the realization of the deep internal moods and conceptions of devotional service embedded within the mantra that are received by the surrendered recipient irrespective of receiving the mantra formally. Ours is a bhagavat-guru-parampara, as we see that Srila Gaura Kisora Das Babaji is not an initiated direct disciple (in pancaratrika system of formally giving mantra along with performing yajna) of Srila Bhaktivinoda Thakura and, similarly Srila Bhaktivinoda Thakura is not a direct disciple of Srila Jagannatha Das Babaji. In the same way, the relationship of our Gurudeva Srila Bhaktivedanta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja and Srila Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada, is in the bhagavat-guru-parampara, carrying the internal moods of the parampara. So anyone who believes they are not connected is only fooling himself.

It was in Vrindavan in the year 2005 around the time of guru-purnima, when after having darshan of Srila Prabhupada’s samadhi in ISKCON Vrindavan, I went on the morning walk with Srila Gurudeva. At that time Gurudeva told me, “You know, I am the first disciple of Swami Prabhupada as I gave him samadhi and performed last rites according to his wishes, and in our Sanatana Dharma it is well known that the eldest son performs the last rites of his father.”

There is another pastime that is very candid in my memory. I was walking with Gurudeva at Govardhana during the morning walk and his hand was on my shoulder. During the morning walks, he usually looked for me to ask any questions. A dog who was severely wounded and bleeding happened to pass by. That day Gurudeva himself was not feeling well and the moment I saw that dog, Gurudeva pressed my shoulder and pulled me close to him as if to prevent me from the sight of the wounded dog. Gurudeva said, “Don’t look at that dog.” Ten minutes later he sat on the roadside bench and looked at me in the eye. There were other senior devotees also. Now I knew he was going to answer the question that was coming in my heart. He started by quoting from Mukunda Mala Stotra by King Kulasekhar.

idam sariram sata-sandhi-jarjaram
pataty avasyam parinama-pesalam
kim ausadham prcchasi mudha durmate
niramayam krsna-rasayanam piba
–Sri Mukunda-mala stotra by Sri Kulasekhara (37)

Gurudeva quoted this full sloka, and in a very serious mood told me, “This world is full of pains and miseries. If you try to see them you will be bewildered, so better just chant your rounds on your mala attentively. This body is surely going to fall prey to diseases and pains; and all the joints in the body will slacken and break. Death and old age are sure to come and no medical advancement can save us. O fool, which medicine are you taking to save yourself? Don’t waste time, take shelter of Radha-Krishna and constantly drink the nectar of Their holy names.”

O Gurudeva, I can still feel your hand pressing my shoulder, taking my focus away from the scorching fire of material existence and guiding me towards the path of Rupanuga bhakti.

sri rupera krpa yena ama prati haya
se-pada asraya yara sei mahasaya
prabhu lokanatha kabe sange lana yabe
sri-rupera pada-padme more samarpibe
–"Suniyachi Sadhu Mukhe" by Srila Narottama Das Thakura (Verses 3-4)

I pray again and again that the mercy of Sri Rupa Gosvami may shower down upon me. Oh, what a wonder! One who has attained the shelter of the lotus feet of Srila Rupa Gosvami is indeed most fortunate. When will my Srila Gurudeva, Srila Lokanatha Gosvami, take me with him to meet Sri Rupa Gosvami and offer me at his lotus feet?

prabhu narayana kabe sange lana yabe
sri-rupera pada-padme more samarpibe

When will my Srila Gurudeva, Srila Narayana Gosvami Maharaja, take me with him to meet Sri Rupa Gosvami and offer me at his lotus feet?

Even while teaching the basic principle of the reality of life, Srila Gurudeva was fully absorbed at every moment in the service moods of a maidservant of Radhaji as Ramana Manjari under the guidance of Sri Rupa Manjari. He clearly said you should do your sincere bhajana and remember that your Sri Guru is waiting for you outside Radha and Krishna’s kunja.

Endless times He would recite the verse:

yat-kinkarisu bahusah khalu kaku-vani
nityam parasya purusasya sikhanda-mauleh
tasyah kada rasa-nidher vrsabhanu-jayas
tat-keli-kunja-bhavanangana-marjani syam
–Sri Radha-rasa-sudha-nidhi by Srila Prabodhananda Sarasvati (8)

O Radhaji! You are an ocean of rasa. The supreme enjoyer Krishna is falling at the feet of your maidservants and humbly begs them to allow Him to enter Your kunja, which all the manjaris are carefully guarding. Will I be able to become a stick in the broom used by the sakhis to clean that kunja?

O Gurudeva, please be merciful upon me so that I can attain a drop of the nectar that you were freely distributing. Being a wretched and fallen person, I could not take even a drop. Save me, Gurudeva!

I heard from your lotus mouth many many times what Srila Raghunatha das Gosvami has said:

tavaivasmi tavaivasmi na jivami tvaya vina
iti vijnaya devi tvam naya mama caranantikam
–Sri Vilapa Kusumanjali by Srila Raghunata Das Gosvami (96)

“O Radhe! I am Yours, I am Yours! I will not be able to maintain my life without Your shelter. Knowing this please bring me in Your shelter.”

O Gurudeva! You are Ramana Manjari. Please accept me in your service. Without you I have no shelter, no shelter, in all the three world.

It was my fortune to be with Srila Gurudeva on one morning walk in Mathura at Dampier Nagar Park near our Sri Kesavaji Gaudiya Matha. While walking, suddenly Gurudeva bent down, picked a yellow flower and put it behind his ear. He looked at me and smiled. I was wondering why Srila Gurudeva, a sannyasi, did this, as many people who were out for their morning walk were greeting him. After he sat down, he told us that Krishna likes these flowers and puts them on His ear to attract the gopis. Moreover its colour is golden yellow, which reminds Him of Radhaji. Then He quoted a sloka from Bhagavatam in which the gopis glorify the beauty of Krishna:

barhapitam nata-vara-vapuh karnayoh karnikaram
bibhrad vasah kanaka-kapisam vaijayantim ca malam
–Srimad Bhagavatam (10.21.5)

I was unaware of this verse at that time, but when I read 10th canto of Srimad Bhagavatam, I found this verse where the karnika flower is mentioned. I could see that Srila Gurudeva, while walking, sitting or preaching, was always absorbed in the mood of the Vraja gopis.

O Gurudeva, your smiling face with that karnika flower on your ear, will I be able to see it again?

Today, I pray to Srila Gurudeva and everyone in his spiritual family to bless me so that I can follow and practice even a tiny fraction of the internal moods of Srila Gurudeva. This is my heartfelt humble puspanjali at the lotus feet of Srila Gurudeva on the occasion of his centennial appearance.

The fallen servant,
Rohininandan das
