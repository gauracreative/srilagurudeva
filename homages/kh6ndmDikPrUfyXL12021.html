Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Nona Pilot

India, Gurugram

Srila Gurudeva ki jaya ho!

I offer my humble obeisances at the lotus feet of my beloved Gurudeva, Sri Srimad Bhaktivedanta Narayana Goswami Maharaja

I met my Srila Gurudeva for the first time in the year 2001 and I was very nervous. I met him at the Keshavji Gaudiya Math in Mathura, in his room on the top floor. As I walked into his room, he turned around to look at me. Light shined on his deep-blue eyes and he said, "Do you see all that is around you? Krishna can destroy this in one second then the other second he can bring it all back." These were the first words I heard from him without any introduction of me to him. His words struck like lightning, they pierced in my heart with the same faith as he spoke them. That moment I became so jubilant in my heart, I knew that I had found the true Guru! He gave me a “special” harinama mala as he called it, and I started chanting my 16 rounds.

On one Guru Purnima day, I lovingly bought a 10-rupee, pink, lotus flower for Srila Gurudeva. I was very excited to give it to him. It was a hot and sultry day, I stood in the line for more than one hour, patiently holding the lotus in my right hand and covering it with my left hand from the man in front of me who was drenched in sweat. When my turn came to pay my obeisance, I swayed away from the chair where Srila Gurudeva was sitting because I got pushed away from Gurudeva by the crowd. My heart just sank thinking that I missed the chance of giving this lotus to him. All of a sudden, I saw Srila Gurudeva get up on his knees and grab the flower from my hand, and offered it to the picture of his Gurudeva, Srila Bhakti Prajnana Kesava Gosvami Maharaja, behind him. How he knew my heart, it was so overwhelming.

A year went by and I was chanting regularly. A desire came in my heart to take diksha (second initiation) from Gurudeva but I wanted that he should give me diksha without me asking him. I must have cried for six months longing to get diksha. Gurudeva returned from the west and he was in the new Gopinath Bhavan Gaudiya Matha in Vrindavan. As I went to take his darshan, he asked me, “Have I given you diksha yet?” I replied, “No Gurudeva.” “Come to my room tomorrow morning I want to give you diksha.” I started dancing in my heart. I know those of you who are reading this must be thinking, “Oh yes, that happened to me too.” Yes, so was our Gurudeva who read all of our hearts. Even during Harikatha when he spoke to so many devotees, he could understand our heart. He touched the exact topic that you needed clarification on. What wonderful association we have had with him. He joked with us, he scolded us, and yet, he loved us.

Once, Gurudeva was staying at our house and I just came from a family-function. I saw Gurudeva sitting in our living room and quickly sneaked upstairs to change my outfit. When I came downstairs to Gurudeva, he looked at me raising his hand to his ears and laughingly said in Hindi, “Kahan gaye woh ek-ek kilo ke jo kaan mai pehane the?” (Oh, where are those one-kg big-big earrings you had on when you ran upstairs?)". Then I replied (in embarrassment), “Guruji aap toh sab dekh lete hain” (Gurudeva, you can see everything). He laughed even louder. It was so sweet.

Srila Gurudeva was staying at our house and he was in his room upstairs. I was downstairs in the living room and a big bumblebee started flying round and round around my one-year-old daughter. All the bramacaris were resting and I did not know how to get rid of it. I was so afraid that it would sting my daughter. Feeling helpless, I just prayed to Gurudeva to please get rid of this bee, and when I opened my eyes after 5 seconds, the bee was lying dead right next to my daughter’s foot. I knew for sure that Gurudeva had saved my daughter from his room upstairs.

I had the opportunity as a pilot to fly Gurudeva from Jammu to Delhi, and once from Delhi to Calcutta. He was never interested in seeing the cockpit and did not eat anything on the plane. On the Calcutta flight, I went back from the cockpit to the business class to check up on him. In the business class there were not any other passengers except Pujyapad Madhava Maharaja and Gurudeva. When I reached Gurudeva’s seat, he sweetly said, “Why is your name Nona? It means “salty” in Bengali why is it not sweet?” Then the three of us just started laughing.

Once, at my house in Gurgaon, a lady devotee came to meet Gurudeva and said, “Guruji, I lost my harinaam mala!” Gurudeva looked at her and asked, “... arre tumne apne kaan ki bali toh kabhi khoye nahi?” (Oh but you have never lost your earrings?)

Another time a different lady came inquisitively and asked, “Guruji do you read people’s hands?” Initially, Gurudeva ignored her, but when she asked this for the third time, Gurudeva just looked at her and said, “Nahi, par mai haatho ki liney badal deta hun” (No. But I change the lines of the hands, meaning, that he can change your destiny). This was our bonafide Guru who was so powerful and so pure. I pray in his lotus feet that he may always come to bless me and my family so that we may never give up chanting.

My last interaction with Gurudeva was in 2010, when I went to see him off at the Delhi airport when he was flying to Bhubaneswar. I helped Pujyapad Brajnath Prabhu and Srila Madhava Maharaja with check-in process. Gurudeva was in a wheelchair. He was very quiet. I walked with them to the waiting area. Sripad Brajnath Prabhu put Srila Gurudeva on a recliner chair so that he could rest a little before the flight. Sunlight fell bright on his divine body, the rays from the sun made his body glow. Gurudeva was sleeping, and I really wanted him to look at me, but I decided not to disturb him. However, when I looked up at him after doing my pranams, Gurudeva, knowing my heart, opened his eyes just enough so I could say my last goodbye to him. I stared at him knowing in my heart that this is his last glance on me in this birth.

His compassionate eyes blessed me and I walked away with so much pain in my heart.

Srila Gurudeva - blessing my daughter

Your dear daughter,
Nona Jangeed (or 'Nona Pilot' as I am commonly known in our Gaudiya Matha)
