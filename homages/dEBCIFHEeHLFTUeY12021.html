Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Damodara Priya Dasi

Australia, Gold Coast

Srila Bhaktivedenta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja.

100th Centenary Vyasa Puja Offering to His Divine Grace Srila Bhaktivedanta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja.

I offer my humble obeisances at the lotus feet of Srila Gurudeva.

Some practice but do not preach, others preach but do not practice, but one who is perfect in both preaching and practice is the guru of the entire universe. You are a real jagad-guru, for you practice what you preach.[1]

1. BACKGROUND

I first had the fortune of meeting Srila Gurudeva in or around 1996 -1997, when I was a teenager completing secondary school in Murwillumbah, Australia. Instantly, Srila Gurudeva took my heart and put it in his pocket. My mother, Malati Dasi, stepfather Balarama Das, and three siblings, Shyama Dasa, Gopali Dasi, and Pushpasara Dasi also had this opportunity. I recall Srila Gurudeva always glorified Srila A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada and humbly lay everything at the feet of his guru-parampara.

There are many profound memories from this time. Memories of tears. Memories of calling out. Memories of finally sensing something bigger than ourselves. Memories of extreme joy and fulfilment. There are also memories that are sweet in their simplicity. For example, once I met with Srila Gurudeva in India during Kartika and I observed to him, “Gurudeva, I am such a donkey” (many will be aware of the analogy that Srila Gurudeva makes between donkey behaviours and our clumsy attempts to exploit the environment for short-lived gratification). He replied “Yes, but I am so happy to have a donkey like you”. Such was (is!) the affection of this extraordinary soul for all His sons and daughters, in spite of our (my) flaws.

In time, Srila Gurudeva gave his blessing to me to study law, which I did. Srila Gurudeva wrote to me indicating such a path would help my logic and would be akin to “having very strong arms” and that “I should speak so boldly” in the service of Sri Sri Radha Krsna. He also instructed that I read Jaiva-dharma at least a hundred times and suggested that one day I would help in his publishing department. I pray these blessings will manifest one lifetime. Srila Gurudeva was firm with many of his daughters in Australia (and other places) that they should not merely be meek. Instead, they should be strong and brave.

Srila Gurudeva also opened my eyes and hearts to nuances in the philosophy that had previously been entirely unknown to me. I consider examples of these below.

2. TEACHINGS

By way of example, Srila Gurudeva taught us the distinction between vaidhi and raganuna-bhakti.

The distinction between vaidhi and raganuga-bhakti

In law, we often speak of both the substantive and procedural requirements of justice. There is no justice in a finding of guilt if the procedural requisites of a fair trial are not given. In many ways, this is analogous to the distinction between vaidhi and raganuga-bhakti. The distinction is important to the articulation of the difference between the goal and the method we use to achieve that goal. They are different, but the denial of one renders the other useless. There is no procedure without substance and no substance without procedure. One of Srila Gurudeva’s many inconceivable gifts is an explanation of this distinction and the bestowing of a process by which we can serve him, and consequently, his beloved. In deciphering the roadmap of our spiritual journey, Srila Gurudeva taught us that we must know the both the goal and the process by which that goal is to be achieved. Srila Gurudeva took great care to ensure our understanding of the importance of our ‘prajoyana’.

The strict instruction not to criticise

By way of further example, Srila Gurudeva consistently gave the strict instruction not to criticise. Mediating on the faults of others destroys the creeper of devotion and hardens our hearts. He taught that we must be ferocious in our loyalty and determination to serve, but compassionate always in our dealing with others. He taught us that one who performs duties meticulously, but engages in criticism of others gives no justice to the performance of any limb of bhakti. Many times, Srila Gurudeva told the story of ‘the prostitute and the brahmana’: a prostitute who meditated only on the virtue of the brahmana gained more spiritual advancement in so doing than the brahmana who contemplated the fallen nature of the prostitute.

Once, in my immaturity, I expressed some concern about the practices of another person. I did not directly criticise the person as such, but my mental intent was likely coming from a place of judgment. Srila Gurudeva’s response was, in essence, for me to mind my own business. Srila Gurudeva said that people are free to come and go, and that only by progressing in our own bhakti are we able to help others. This is different, of course, to tolerating impropriety when it comes to safety, standing up for the vulnerable, and so forth, but, other than that, it is not for us to find fault unnecessarily.

Damodara Priya Dasi.

Articulation of the reasons for Mahaprabhu’s descent

By way of a final (but not exhaustive) example, it can be observed that Srila Gurudeva also went to great lengths to encourage us to be able to clearly articulate the many reasons for Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu’s descent. Primarily, he instructed that Sri Krsna took the hue of Srimati Radhika and descended as Mahaprabhu for three main reasons: 1) Desiring to understand the glory of Radharani’s love, 2) the wonderful qualities in Him that She alone relishes through Her love, and 3) the happiness She feels when She realizes the sweetness of His love.

As stated in the Caitanya-caritamrita: "Caitanya Mahaprabhu inaugurated the sankirtana movement. One who worships Him through sankirtana is fortunate indeed. Such a person is truly intelligent, whereas others, who have but a poor fund of knowledge, must endure the cycle of repeated birth and death. Of all sacrificial performances, the chanting of the Lord’s holy name is the most sublime." [2]

3. CLOSING THOUGHTS: LOVE AND AFFECTION

Here, I have reflected only briefly on only a handful of examples of the profound teachings of Srila Gurudeva. There are so many more, of course. The point being - for current purposes - is that Srila Gurudeva bestowed on us a strong framework and “road-map” by which to navigate our journey.

Most importantly, however, Srila Gurudeva gave (gives!) us unmatched love and affection. In so doing, we are instructed on the importance of mood: ferocity in compassion and compassion in ferocity. We often use the paternal description of Srila Gurudeva as our father. In many ways, He is. Srila Gurudeva certainly used the paternal form of address for us, his “dear darling daughters and sons”. Srila Gurudeva, of course, is also so much more than that. As observed in the Caitanya-mangala:

Under ordinary circumstances one’s own father and mother are worshipable, but in every species one gets a father and mother. Far rarer is to get guru and Krsna. The spiritual master can bestow prema-bhakti to those who have attained his mercy, therefore he is the topmost father, mother, and friend of everyone.[3]

Further, as stated in the Bhagavad-gita:

Know the acarya to be as good as Myself. Never disrespect him. Never envy him or consider him an ordinary man for He is the sum total of all the demigods.[4]

In closing, in this, the 100th appearance of my Srila Gurudeva, it must be conceded that it is near impossible to truly glorify the extent of the gifts bestowed upon us by our Srila Gurudeva. Nonetheless, it is our duty to try. To that end, this offering has reflected on examples of Srila Gurudeva’s teachings from our parampara (such as the distinction between vaidhi and raganuga-bhakti; the importance of identifying our goal; the strict instruction not to criticise; and the articulation of the reasons for Mahaprabhu’s descent).

I have also attempted to reflect on his profound kindness to us all: A teacher like no other, a guardian like no other, an advocate like no other, and a vestibule of kindness like no other.

Thank you to the organisers of this centenary for the opportunity to make this offering.

All faults in this offering are entirely my own.

Your servant,

Damodara-priya Dasi (Australia)

[1] Caitanaya-caritamrita, Antya lila, 4.102, 103.

[2] Caitanaya-caritamrita Adi 7.23, 24.

[3] Madhya-khanda.

[4] Srimad Bhagavatam 11.17.27.
