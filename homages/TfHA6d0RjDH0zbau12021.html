Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Jaishree devi dasi

United States, North Boston

First of all I offer my most heartfelt millions of millions of dandavat pranamas, and all my affection at the lotus feet of my most worshipful and beloved Srila Gurudeva - nitya-lila pravista-om-visnupada Sri Srimad Bhaktivedanta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja. And the same unlimited obeisances to my grandfather-guru Srila Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada. And the same unlimited koti-pranamas to our parama-gurudeva Srila Bhakti Prajnana Kesava Gosvami Maharaja, as well to our jagat-guru Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Saraswati Srila Prabhupada, and Srila Bhativinoda Thakura Prabhupada and so on before to the our most incredible mission leader of this kali-yuga era Sri Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu and His associates. All Glories to the Vaisanvas!

All glories to Srila Gurudeva, as we enter his centennial year, who left this world, my sisters brothers and I, 10 year ago today. A day and a shift I will never forget, and the source of my tears. And this tiny, pathetic but fully present offering of devotion and memory, as it may please the Vaisnavas who I offer all my dandavat pranamas and affection to all over the world. Hare Krsna!

I have always been close with God. I have always been somewhat obsessed with God, actually, which I now know has a word to describe in sanskrit - mamata.

When I was little, I envisioned God to be like Abraham Lincoln with a white beard, sitting cross legged on a cloud. And in this moment, I can say that was the image of my Srila Gurudeva. In fact, the first time I saw my Srila Gurudeva, and that image, was in a book called Jaiva Dharma. I had been invited to a Sunday class by my doctor / acupuncturist Jonathan aka Nimai Caitanya Das. We would be meeting to sing ‘some songs, read from a book, and have some vegetarian food’. It sounded good to me. The conversation, which I have shared in my books and with friends went like this:

Nimai and I during a healing session:

"So you’re teaching yoga full time now, yes?" “Yes” I answer. “And you are chanting some mantras? You’re doing some kirtana?” He asks. "Yes”, I say, “I’ve been learning about (such and such divine personalities of God in Hinduism) and chanting (such and such mantras to Lord Shiva etc)” "Uh huh” he says “And you’re not eating any meat or drinking alcohol, right?” "Yes”, I say “I went vegan on New Years’ day 2008 and haven’t had (such and such or such) for quite a while...”
He pauses."So…” he says “do you want to go deeper?”

Yes. Yes I did.

He told me the book was a conversation about being devoted in the world, and that it would hopefully satisfy my desire to embody more than I had in my previous religious studies. And so it was that I found myself sitting with a small group one Sunday in early October 2008, opening this book by a saint householder named Srila Bhaktivinoda Thakura, and seeing an image of my Gurudeva for the first time.
I remember saying "Oh, there’s my Gurudeva".
I said that despite never having heard that word before that day.

And that was it.
I had no history of ‘the movement’ or teachers or personalities in this line of yoga. But it was like God had shown up for the first time in the form of embodied personality, embodied spirituality, as well as embodied “religion”. It all came together and made sense, for real and forever.

When I met my Srila Gurudeva in Houston TX, I was ridiculous. I kept running to the bathroom. My entire life, all 30 years, all the education and travel and career was just like mud. It was a basic foundation upon which I could build. And my Gurudeva was about to take it all - all my karma, all my debt, all my tears, all my sadness and confusion, in exchange for one simple free vow.

That visit, when Nimai Caitanya Das brought us in to see Gurudeva, after I had taken my first brahmana-diksa initiation, Nimai told Srila Gurudeva what I was doing for a living aka with my life. I don’t remember if he mentioned that I had been an actor, but he did say I left everything to teach yoga. And that I aspired to share bhakti through my voice and my work now.

My Gurudeva said simply - in the language that I could understand, “Good, Good. Keep doing. Very good.”

That was it. A side to side nod, and a flick of his lotus eyes. All the instruction was there. On another journey to be with Gurudeva in CA, after my first time in India, I received the first life-altering darsana of Srila Gurudeva’s crystal blue eyes. I was floored. I was seared through my soul in a way that I will never forget. Nor will it be matched.

Again, I was so ridiculous. I always messed up. I was always forgetting to do something properly. Feeling immensely unqualified (still), but knowing that my relationship would never be understood in the material realm anyway. That our unconditional eternal bond would never be revealed or explained, or even questioned. But that it was just how it was. And what I can speak to is WHO Srila Gurudeva has sent to me in the embodied form of his teachings, his family - my siksa-gurus.

Of all the many secrets revealed in Vaisnava dharma, etiquette, Vedic literature, spoken and written text - the highest and most regarded is LOVE. True, rich, free, undeniable LOVE comes through the devotees. And one way this love comes through is via the understanding that we are an siksa-disciplic lineage. Not only diksa. We are both. It’s truly a both-and-world.

The devotees, the servants of the servants, are the most reliable, relishable, and precious jewels of Guru. Guru means heavy - the one who brings wisdom to the ignorant. While I may stay ignorant, I do appreciate the heavy. And some of my siksa teachers - all of them actually, are very heavy at times. I get chastised appropriately. But they all love me. They all serve my Gurudeva more than I could ever truly appreciate. They all have their own relationships with him and his devotees, and serve him as Sri Sri Radha’s goddess gang.

Yes, we are a tribe of Goddess worshipping light working, soft hearted and passionate souls, one of the many secrets and tricks of divinity in the material world.

At this incredibly polarized time in history, we are being tested. At this very moment where relationships are so strained, those of us without an anchor may become victims to powers and pressures that may not conform to our deepest desires. What I have learned is that we are made of desire. So it’s quite important, actually, that we cleanse the mirror of our hearts and realize our dreams. I personally don’t feel good - I mean I feel deeply distressed - when I am not actualizing my dreams into reality. Bhakti yoga has taught me the science of how to manifest from a place of truth, as opposed to the matrix of lies of this material world. And that has helped me tremendously in navigating the pain and choppy waters of life. Especially when it comes to relationships.

Most devotees’ hearts are soft like mine. But because I am in a line of Lions, because I understand who my Gurudeva is - through his disciples, his loves, his family, his children - in the form of so many beautiful souls supporting and leading God’s children towards our highest and happiest way of being - I am supported in my truth. My dharma, my life path and my work is supported by the science of self-realization. The science of the unbreakable soul.
Buzzing around like a bumble bee offering whatever I can, in every moment, despite my ignorance, brilliance or in-between psychology, shows me that the truth of who we are has nothing to do with education. It proves to me that divine qualities are inherent, and often misguided by karma and struggle and story. And that pain, even my lifelong pain, is just a teacher in itself.

And mostly, I have learned that unconditional love is very hard to experience in the material world. I am so blessed to have my parents, they are pure and humble and good. And so are most souls. Even when covered.

Bhakti yoga is a repeatable, relatable, and reliable science and it’s all about guru-nistha. It’s about the principles of living a balanced life dedicated to something and someONE. It’s the most intelligent practice because it exemplifies a toroidal energy of devotion and service.

I am so happy to have my Srila Gurudeva. I get to unwrap the present that is my relationship with him every day, in every breath. And I get to judge my life on my consciousness of serving him. Even though he may have left soon after that physical glance, He lives on not only in the non-changing practices of our lineage - but in his devotees. And that is the one thing I can hold onto, even at the time of death. Because we take absolutely nothing material with us at that time. None of this comes with us. Only relationships, only love will come with us.

That is my simple realization of Gurudeva. That’s why I named my daughter after my Gurudeva. That is my greatest awakening. And the only wish I can have for humanity. To realize what Guru actually means. And to serve.

My millions of koti-pranamas,

All my affection,

Your servant,

Jaishree Devi Dasi
