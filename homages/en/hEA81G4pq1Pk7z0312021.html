Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Subal Sakha Das

United States, Lake Worth, Florida

om ajnana-timirandhasya jnananjana-salakaya
caksur unmilitam yena tasmai sri-gurave namah

I was born in the darkest of ignorance, and my spiritual master opened my eyes with the torch light of knowledge. I offer my respectful obeisances unto him.

First, I would like to offer my respectful obeisances at the lotus of my diksa-guru, my spiritual master, Srila Bhaktivedanta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja and equally to my siksa Guru, Srila A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada.

Dear Srila Gurudeva,

Please except my respectful obeisances

All glories to Srila Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada

All glories to Srila Bhakti Prajana Kesava Gosvami Maharaja

Today on this very auspicious vyasa-puja celebration I would like to take the opportunity to express my heartfelt gratitude and appreciation to you, and offer my puspanjali at your lotus feet. This year is particularly special as it is your One Hundred Year Centennial and so many devotees around the world are also offering their heartfelt glorifications to you.

In many lifetimes I could not repay you for how much you have touched my life, and for all that you have given me. My life is certainly so much better off for knowing you, and for having your divine association. I only hope that in some small way I can repay you, by helping you in your mission to spread Krishna consciousness, and to share with others the gift that you have given to me.

Before I met you, I was going through a very dark period in my life.

I was very addicted to taking drugs and the drugs had taken over my life.

Because of my addiction, I had given up the desire to live. I am very grateful that Krishna blessed my life and gave me the willingness to get free from drug addiction a little over 23 years ago. When I stopped taking drugs, I started to rebuild my life and incorporated all the good things that I had pushed aside due to my addiction.

It was at that point that I started attending the ISKCON temple in Los Angeles once again, and continued reading Srila Prabhupada's books, which eventually led me to meeting you.

Everything was going along nicely at ISKCON, however one day, I noticed there were flyers all around the community with your photo, but the comments on the flyers were very offensive and many devotees were making so many false acquisitions about you.

I am grateful that I saw those flyers and heard those comments, because it made me even more curious to come and meet you. I am very grateful that at the same time, Krishna put some very special devotees into my life who spoke of your glories and greatness.

I would meet with these devotees regularly and they shared so many wonderful things about you, about your relationship with Srila Prabhupada, about your mission, and how you cared for Srila Prabhupada’s disciples and inspired them in their spiritual life.

I heard that very soon you would be attending the upcoming harikatha festival in Badger, California, and I was very excited at the chance to meet you and have your darsana.

When I first saw you, it was during a small darsana in your room in Nirguna prabhu’s home. When I was introduced to you, you were happy to hear that I came to see you, even though so many from ISKCON told me “Do not go and see Narayana Maharaja.” But actually, it is only by your causeless mercy that I was able to come. It was there in Badger that a new journey had begun for me.

What a special moment it was when the evening program was about to begin and I saw you walk from your room for the first time heading to the pandala to give your evening class.

There was so much excitement in the air and everyone’s hearts were overflowing with love as you gazed at them with your sweet glances and loving exchanges.

What a glorious moment to witness. This is perhaps what I miss the most, is the outpouring of love that always surrounded you whenever you were near.

When you sat on your vyasa-asana and bhajanas were being sung, it was as if I was transported back to some ancient moment in time.

Before you spoke, you glorified your Gurudeva, Srila Bhakti Prajana Kesava Gosvāmī Maharaja, and you offered your heartfelt pranamas equally to Srila Prabhupada.

There was so much emotion in your voice, and at that moment, I felt the genuine love and affection that you and Srila Prabhupada shared. Right at that moment, I knew that all the negative things I was hearing in ISKCON were false.

Your harikatha pierced my heart as you began to speak of the glories of Srila Prabhupada, Sri Krishna, and of your beloved Srimati Radhika.

I knew right then and there that I accepted you in my heart.

From the very first time I met you I had a camera in my hand and I was capturing so many photographs hoping to utilize these photos in service to you and your preaching mission. From the very beginning, you gave me your blessings to perform this service. I will always be very grateful for the opportunity to have rendered some small service to you throughout the years.

I feel very humbled to have had the opportunity to set up several photo-shoots with you, and I am honoured that the photos that were taken were utilized in front of many books and in many temples and preaching centres around the world.

I remember when I saw you in Navadvipa-dhama several months later, I approached you to offer flowers to you and Srila Vamana Gosvāmī Maharaja.

In the middle of me speaking, you interrupted me and said, “I want you to go to Vrndavana, and I want you to take photos for a photo book on Vraja.” You called me to your room several times in the next few days to speak about the project and you made all arrangements for me to go to Vrndavana.

I can never repay you for giving me that personal invitation to go to Vraja. What a wonderful way to enter into the holy dhama by instruction from your spiritual master. It was only by your causeless mercy that this occurred. I know that you wanted this book to be published so that all can realize the beauty of this sacred place. I am still working on this book years later, and by your blessings and by your mercy I hope this book will come soon.

I will always be grateful for that first meeting with you at the New Braja Festival in Badger, California, and so grateful for the guidance, love, and kindness you have always shown me.

In many lifetimes, I can never thank you for all that you have given to me in my life. You have touched my life so deeply.

I feel very fortunate that Krishna arranged for me to meet you, and that I had the great fortune to meet someone as extraordinary as you. My life will be changed forever as a result of this meeting. I pray to Sri Guru & Gauranga that I can always be engaged in your service.

Just as the Yamuna River flows, your boundless love & causeless mercy flows into all of our lives.

With much gratitude forever,

aspiring to serve the Vaisnavas,

Subal Sakha Das

(Photographer)
