Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Vishnu Swami

United States, Florida

Srila Bhaktivedenta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja.

“Whatever Happens, I Will Not Take Initiation!”

My Srila Gurudeva was famous as a thief.

A thief of hearts.

But I didn't believe it…

...until His tricky ways captured my heart and ruined my life forever.

It was the month of Kartika in 1998 and my heart was turning ritvik.

A lifetime in ISKCON…

...and years of living in the “belly of the beast”, ISKCON Headquarters in Sri Mayapura Dhama…

...left me doubting that a Guru as pure and powerful as Srila Prabhupada would ever grace this earth again.

It was vacation time from Mayapura Gurukula, and our place of play was Vrindavan Dhama.

My birth father was visiting that year, so I stayed with him and therefore was…

...unavoidably brought to Srila Gurudeva’s temples during Vrajamandala Parikarma.

This was not surprising because, even though my father is Prabhupada Disciple, he was and is an avid follower of our Srila Gurudeva.

He and other Srila Gurudeva followers used to tease me by saying,

“When are you going to take initiation”.

And I would laugh at them inside.

They knew nothing of my hardened and stubborn heart.

I was committed.

I told myself,

‘No matter what happens, I will not take initiation”

Too many times had I seen Gurus fail.

Too many times had I seen through the naivete of overzealous disciples elevating conditioned souls to the status of Guru…

...and taking that misplaced faith…

...which perhaps sprouted from:

- a cultish need to belong

- unresolved daddy issues

- or a need for moral superiority

...and shoving it in the face of any possible new recruit in their vicinity.

These “Gaudiya Matha People” were not going to get me.

I would not fall for this Guru…

...just because he was old, Indian, charismatic, and was hard to understand.

“Not me”, my ego said.

I was smarter than that.

Boy was I wrong.

It was not the social pressure that got me.

Nor the need to belong, or to feel safe in my spiritual life.

I was already the “golden child” in ISKCON Mayapura…

...supposedly a demigod incarnated (as all us Gurukulis were told).

My community was just fine.

It was rich, powerful, and making a difference in the world.

My material self-interest did not need to join these bearded Gaudiya Matha folk…

...who could not seem to even get it together to clean their small temples.

It was Srila Gurudeva Himself who captured me.

In a private darshan, I asked Him a sincere but challenging question.

“How does one identify a bonafide Spiritual Master?”

Here replied,

“You cannot.

A conditioned soul is unable to recognize a pure devotee.

The process is you find a devotee who is following properly. Who is chanting nicely, following all scriptures and he will lead you to your Guru”.

I was not convinced so I said,

“That’s what I did, and that’s how I got a bogus Guru who fell.”

He said,

“Then how did you come to me?”

I said,

“My father brought me.”

What Srila Gurudeva said next was one of the most impactful moments of my life…

...and reveals what I consider the primary truth of what it means to be Guru.

He said,

“No!

Krishna brought you to me.

Your and my relationship is eternal…

...nothing could ever come between us”.

At that moment it’s like I was hit with a divine hurricane of feeling.

It was as if what He said was the only real truth I had ever heard in my life.

The “thief” had won.

My heart was stolen.

A love sparked within that felt so new…

…but yet was the most familiar and real feeling in the world.

Something within was awakened…

...and my life was destroyed forever.

Now I was His slave and nothing else in the world mattered.

The next day I was initiated.

And shortly after that, I gave up everything that I knew.

I left my family, community and friends…

...and dedicated myself to spending every possible moment with my Srila Gurudeva.

I traveled with Him for years…

...until He broke my heart.

One year after Vrajamandala Parkirma I came to My Srila Gurudeva with another plan for how I would continue to travel and serve Him.

He said,

“Why waste the time? Better do something useful”

He then went on to elaborate about all the other services and preaching I could do.

This made no sense to me.

How could directly serving Guru and being close to Him not be “useful” and be a “waste of time”?

My heart was shattered.

But my heart is His, and He has the right to do what he wants with it.

I fell into depression for about 6 months before starting to realize the wisdom of His words.

Guru and disciples' relationship is eternal.

He told me that when we first met.

It is not separated by time and space.

And if we are truly going to advance in our connection and service to Sri Guru…

...we must be able to connect with Him at all times.

We know from Archana (deity worship) and the sloka,

“Prathamam tu gurum pujya”

that anything we offer to Krsna or any seva we do, must first be given to Sri Guru.

Only after receiving the blessings and guidance of Sri Guru do we offer that thing or service to Sri Krsna.

If we are truly a dedicated disciple…

...then anything we receive is first given to Sri Guru.

We cannot eat, drink, sleep, wear clothes or even breathe without His sanctioning this action.

We received this direct training when in the physical presence of my Srila Gurudeva.

As brahmacaris we were trained that if anyone gives us literally anything…

... a penny, a dhoti, a banana etc…

...we would go to Srila Gurudeva’s room and give it to Him.

My Srila Gurudeva would then touch it, to sanctify it and give it back.

This was common practice.

Whenever we would leave to travel…

...even if it was on His direct order and when He paid for the trip…

...we would go do pranams and get permission before departing to the airport or train.

Even when on tour with Srila Gurudeva…

...if we were to travel from one destination to another on separate flights…

...the first course of action upon arriving in the new festival location would be to go to Srila Gurudeva’s room and touch His feet.

This is not something I made up, some cultural thing, or something I heard second hand.

Srila Gurudeva directly taught me this.

On my first tour I failed to tell Srila Gurudeva I had arrived in the new location.

I did not understand why I would need to do this. I was just with Him yesterday and He knew I was coming.

He was furious.

During Srila Gurudeva’s massage that day He told me that He was worried and that whenever arriving, the first thing I should do was to go and see Him.

That is the etiquette of a disciple.

So the point is that, as aspiring disciples we must do everything through Guru first.

And to truly do this we must always be connected to Sri Guru...

...even in times of separation…

...especially in times of separation.

Seva in separation is not fun.

But it is necessary.

Without separation, meeting has no meaning.

We tasted this cycle of meeting (milan) and separation (viraha) in small portions during Srila Gurdeva’s mantifast lila.

But now with Srila Gurudeva’s disappearance from this physical world…

He is teaching us in even deeper and better ways...

...how to serve in separation &...

...how to be connected with Him eternally in a non-physical way.

When we master this lesson,

We will never be alone.

Kali Yuga will progress.

People, even vaishanvas, will quarrel & separate.

Spiritual societies will be corrupted.

Covid may keep us apart.

The world may be engulfed in tyranny.

But…

...we will be happy.

Because we will have our Srila Gurudeva in our hearts...

...closer to us than “The breathe we breath”.

Vaishnavadasanudas,

Vishnu Swami

Srila Gurudeva.
