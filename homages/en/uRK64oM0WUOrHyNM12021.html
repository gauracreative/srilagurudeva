Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Jayadeva Das

United Kingdom, Birmingham

Srila Bhaktivedenta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja.

nama om visnu-padaya radhikayai-priyatmane
sri-srimad bhaktvedanta-narayana iti namine

An offering to Sri Guru Pada Padma Nitya lila Pravista Om Vishnupada Bhaktivedanta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja.

In Srimad Bhagavad Gita it states that anyone, who can recognize the transcendental reality of Bhagavan's janma and activities, shall no longer again take birth in this mortal plane. Similarly in Sri Caitanya Bhagavat, the same divinity and purity attributed to the Supreme Lord in his multifarious incarnations, should be applied likewise, to the appearance day of His pure servants, the suddha bhaktas.

isvarera janma-tithi ye-hena pavitra
vaisnavera sei-mata-tithira caritra
–Sri Caitanya-Bhagavata by Srila Vrindavan Das Thakura (3.48)

Glorification of the descent and appearance of the Vaisnava's therefore is of supreme auspiciousness for the conditioned souls, as they are assisting in the mission of Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu's desire is to reclaim and deliver the suffering jiva's ,from the ill effects of kali yuga. Just as Sri Krsna mercifully appears twenty four times in the year as ekadasi-devi to bestow on the faithful observers varying transcendental fruits according to the differently named days, then we have heard on the appearance day of the Vaisnava's, when we hear of their sublime glories and devotional activities, then their mood of service and surrender also may enter and purify our hearts. On the appearance days of Sakti-tattva and Her expansions our main fasting is through the ears. When during Kartik for instance Srila Gurudeva, those days celebrating the glories of the illustrious and renowned Vaisnava's and Acarya's in our line on their respective appearance and disappearance days, under your direction, would be some of the sweetest moments revealing the true glories of Mahaprabhu's near and dear and our Gaudiya family. In this vein, you also demonstrated the utmost reverence you had for Vaisnava's by deciding to depart from this world on the appearance day of your beloved Godbrother and internal associate Param-puyjapad Srila Bhaktivedanta Vamana Maharaj. Indeed paying homage to him, by not just on the same day, but leaving within the same time period corresponding to his birth time. We also hear from Vaisnava's how you would lovingly, and earnestly, serve all the Gaudiya bhaktas and godbrothers of your beloved Gurudeva when they would visit the Sri Kesavji Gaudiya Matha and elsewhere, thus earning you the affectionately given title/nickname of Bhakta Bhandava from your Gurudeva.

Now we are observing the centennial celebrations of your merciful appearance in this world. On these occasions we are meant to offer the highest eulogies and appreciations of our beloved and revered Srila Gurudeva. Krsna Kripa Sri Murti. If Krsna's full mercy could take an embodied form, it is known as Sri Guru. But how can the words of this lowly person adequately do justice to the magnanimous personality that has lovingly offered entrance to the gateway of bhakti. So thought to try to offer a few words of glorification and personal experiences at your lotus feet on this occasion.

It has been just over ten years since you have become unmanifest from our external vision and these days give us the opportunity to remember, your life history, meeting your own Gurudeva etc, the interactions we would have had with you, and the sublime Hari katha that you allowed to enter our hearts. On a personal note this would have to include the amazing European tours as well as the inestimable good fortune to attend the yearly Vraj Mandala and Gaura Mandala parikrama's, all organised under your direct supervision, and in the association of your beloved disciples and followers that combinedly, created the sweetest of experiences. Reminiscences now some years later, simultaneously invoke feelings of happiness as you made these experiences so tasteful and memorable, but also tinged by mournfulness in the heart, that sadly, such days have now passed. To visit the pastime places in Vrndavan and especially at Goverdhana with you and the parikrama party of devotees would leave anyone feeling eternally indebted and grateful for your kindness in giving these priceless opportunities.

Prior to having first contact with you at the beginning of 1997, I had the supremely good fortune also to have the association and chance to hear from Guru Pada Padma Srila Gaura Govinda Maharaj. Even though with just a relatively few years of association he impressed upon the hearts of the hearers the necessity of sadhu sanga and to hear Krishna Katha directly from the lips of pure Vaisnava's as revealed in all sastra. As such this would allow the spiritual vibration, sabda brahma, to descend via the medium of sound, as well as making a heart connection with one of Krishna's near and dear. This hearing process is eternal he would say. This emphasis he gave, made the transition to your shelter less unnerving due to what now would be an external change of sanga etc after Maharaj's sudden departure at the beginning of 1996. Indeed in the same way you offered the helping hand to the direct disciples of Srila Prabhupada, Bhaktivedanta Swami Maharaj, you likewise welcomed the followers of Srila Gaura Govinda Maharaj into your association, to guide and assist further in their spiritual development. Especially in the first years of your trips outside of india to Europe, many of the places and temples you visited were hosted by devotees favourable to Maharaj, such as in Holland, the UK, France etc.

For my part, this felt so much like his and Krishna's arrangement, as I felt to be somewhat like a lost spiritual orphan after his unexpected leaving. In this connection, at the end of one of his morning classes in Italy on his final European tour of 1995, I remember upon the completion of Krsna katha connected with Vraj lila and the glorifying of the high calibre of the gopi's devotion, Srila Maharaj pronounced that, 'I too am praying to Srila Prabhupada every day that i may become a maidservant of Srimati Radharani'. Such words stayed in the mind as it seemed unusual for him to speak in the first person, during Hari katha. It would appear out of Vaisnava humility he made such a comment, but also maybe can be seen that he was purposefully giving a hint to the fine tuning required of our own aspirations in Krsna bhakti. Maharaj never did whimsical! So when some of his disciples came to you to hear and for further direction, it felt like a natural continuation, for you would be emphasising these very same points, same moods and giving the exact same Gaudiya philosophy and goals, thereby also cementing what was learned before.....for example on jiva tattva, guru tattva etc. Through your nectarean Hari katha and divine prayers of our Rupanuga acaryas, such as Srila Raghunatha das Goswami, you also emphasized the ultimate goal of praying to one day becoming a palya dasi of Srimati Radhika.

Daily you would recite as part of your mangalacharan:

bhajami radham aravinda-netram
smarami radham madhura-smitasyam
vadami radham karuna-bharardram
tato mamanyasti gatir na kapi
–Sri Visakhanandadabhidha-stotram by Srila Raghunatha dasa Gosvami (131)

I Worship Radha who has lotus eyes, I remember Radha who has a sweet smile, and I speak of Radha who is melted with compassion. There is nothing else for me. She is my life and soul.

tavaivasmi tavaivasmi na jivami tvayā vina
iti vijnaya devi tvaṁ naya mam caraṇantikam
–Sri Vilapa Kusumanjali by Srila Raghunatha dasa Gosvami (96)

I am Yours! I am Yours! I cannot live without You! O Devi (Radha), please understand this and bring me to Your lotus feet.

You often would refer to Srila Saraswati Thakur, Bhaktisiddhanta Prabhupada's regret in his latter days of not being able to establish these higher conceptions fully during his manifest pastimes due to other preaching commitments, or as termed, cutting the jungles of mayavada and apasiddhanta. In which case devotees consider, as an offering to your own Gurudeva, that your worldwide preaching for over half a century, which progressively establish these points, has channelled and fulfilled, the mano 'bhistam, the innermost heart desires of Jagad Guru, Srila Prabhupada.

What to speak of elevated Vaisnava's, even to the seemingly unqualified, the lowly in the crowd who just happened to be in attendance (such as myself), you placed so much stress on these aspirations. Famously you would use the example of Princess Diana and how even a penniless, unattractive street beggar could desire her hand in matrimony. You would ask, if this was correct behaviour or not, to crave for the almost impossible? Then you would conclude: that you cannot deny him the right , of his earnest wishes if that was his inner yearning.....and indeed in a future life he most probably will get the opportunity to marry a princess, as is his want! In the same way even the most junior of devotees can have even the minutest of a semblance of longing for this supreme goal as desired by all the Gaudiya Vaisnava's following in the Rupanuga dhara. This seed of desire planted by one of Krishna's own, via the medium of Hari Katha, in and of itself made the unqualified, qualified. This truly is one of your special glories. As Swami Maharaj, Srila Prabhupada would say to his disciples....I am your qualification!

At the same time you would point out that these things are done in a progressive way under proper guidance, step by step. With the heaviness of Guru, you would be strong on the pitfalls of deviating from the proper sequence of development as taught by the spiritual master. As you would often quote and explain from Sri Upadesamrta, placing emphasis that anugatya is required at all times....

tan-nama-rupa-caritadi-sukirtananu-
smrtyoh kramena rasana-manasi niyojya
tisthan vraje tad-anuragi-jananugami
kalam nayed akhilam ity upadesa-saram
–Sri Upadesamrta by Srila Rupa Gosvami (8)

Similarly Srila Rupa Goswami also advises,

sruti-smrti-puranadi-
pancaratra-vidhim vina
aikantiki harer bhaktir
utpatayaiva kalpate
–Sri Bhakti-rasamṛta-sindhu by Srila Rupa Gosvami (1.2.101)

As displayed in your preaching in India or around the world, on many of the topics or sastra to be discussed, would have a systematic aspect about them,(such as Sri Brhad Bhagavatamrta, Sanatan Siksa, Ramananda Samvad, Srimad Bhagavatam, Damodarastakam etc), where starting from the beginning, elementary stages and levels of bhakti and the bhaktas, would advance sequentially to the more elevated stages. All topics as they progressed in depth and gradation would be rich in siddhanta and sastric reference and it would be beautiful for the audience to behold how also you trained your senior disciples to assist you, when you presented these subjects. They were as you said, like your own limbs spreading out, eventually to reach all over the world.

Looking back it is always with constant regret, that in your manifest presence that, due to insincerity and mundane desires, I could never surrender fully to your wishes and follow the instructions given, with only external token gestures offered instead. As such, wasted the valuable opportunity to go closer to you, like I could observe in many of my godbrothers and godsisters who did so. Whether getting encouragement now by reading your books, hearing your classes and also taking inspiration from those surrended sevaka’s who place you in the centre of all their activities, who had so much of your valuable transcendental association, instruction and direct training, and who have so much to offer too, on your behalf. Maybe by accepting the principle of trying to become the servant of the servant....

ahaṁ bhakta-parādhīno
hy asvatantra iva dvija
sādhubhir grasta-hṛdayo
bhaktair bhakta-jana-priyaḥ
–Srimad Bhagavatam (9.4.63)

.....that in some way this also will strengthen the connection with you. Seems we are all wishing to find the nourishment to keep you always in the center , which of course can vary on an individual level in how it is manifest. I pray I can stay faithful to that end.

Srila Gurudeva, your beloved sons and daughters will offer many fragrant flowers to you today, in the form of heartfelt words of love, recollections and truer understandings of your mission in much more detail. Though less aromatic, hopefully you will also accept these few petals of glorification on this special occasion.

We all fondly remember how in the early years of your western preaching you would often entice the audience by sweetly imploring (as only you can), 'Come with me. Come with me to Vrndavan. Leave England, leave Germany, leave Italy(etc), leave your houses and your situations. Instead come with me now, by your ears, to the bank of the Yamuna....'

One day hopefully will be qualified to have the chance to hear your beckoning once again.

Your humble servant,

Jayadeva dasa (UK)

Srila Gurudeva ki jaya!
