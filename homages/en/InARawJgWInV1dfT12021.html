Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Vicitri dasi

India, Vrindavan

nama om visnu-padaya radhikayai-priyatmane
sri-srimad bhaktvedanta-narayana iti namine

O greatest of great yogis, sparkling as you float above the material manifestation, smiling, laughing, sending out beams of love and affection to one and all, opening up Vraja-lila with your eye-witness reports of the daily events going on there, telling us who we really are, revealing the goal so we can do bhajana, encouraging us to see ourselves there, inviting everyone to join you as you serve Yugala-Kisora. “Come live in my heart!” Generous beyond measure, elegance emanating from every pore of your divine body, always in command of every situation, devoid of even a scent of sectarian spirit, capturing everyone with your irresistible charm.

Brajanatha Prabhu tells one story. It was Kartika and we were staying in Varsana. You invited Brajanatha to stay in your room that night. When Brajanatha came to your room to take rest, he was surprised to see that the room was crowded with about 6 people already stretched out on the floor. So he said to you, “Gurudeva, you’re not inconvenienced by having so many people in your room?” You fervently replied, “I would like to have the whole world stay in my room with me!”

Who could imagine anyone so broad-minded! The beauty of the Vaisnava – it cannot be described, it can only be experienced.

The very first time I went to meet you, I was told that you might be busy and I would have to wait quite some time for darsana. But after just a few minutes, the doors to that private kunja magically opened, and there you were, the Moon of Mathura, sitting on your bed, welcoming me and inviting me to come very close to you. Many years later I looked back on this event and got the feeling that you were expecting me, this wayward jiva. Wandering throughout countless universes in trillions of births, I finally experienced for the very first time in all my existence – real love. In this very moment my whole existence became successful. Never before had I met such an open-hearted, exalted personality, someone who understood and cared about my real benefit. Without my even knowing, you snatched me up, put me in your pocket, and claimed me as yours. Oh, how lucky I became! “Wander no more, Beti, you are home. Never again will you ever be alone.”

You played a special role in our parampara, boldly and confidently telling the world about the beauty and sweetness of Vraja and the confidential purpose of Caitanya Mahaprabhu’s mission. Again and again you explained the meaning of sva-bhakti sriyam (the beauty of Srimati Radhika, which is service to Her) and the necessity of hearing about the love between Krsna and the gopis. You told us that 99% of the devotees who come to Mahaprabhu’s line are manjaris by svarupa in madhura-rasa. Srila Vedavyasa Himself manifested the vast Vedas in this world but was despondent until he went into meditation and saw the Vraja-lilas, which he recorded in the 10th Canto for the benefit of the whole world. Only then did he feel satisfied. This proves the utter necessity to hear about the Vraja-lilas. You so delicately and joyfully communicated the selfless love of the Vraja-vasis for Krsna, and encouraged us to begin to see ourselves there in our eternal position as servants of Srimati Radharani under the guidance of Rupa Manjari. You instilled in us the hope that soon we would be able to enter raga-marga and begin to perform real bhajana. You had only the highest expectations for us, and like a loving mother and sensible father, guided each of us personally to keep us on track, all the while pushing us to continue moving along. You urged us not to waste our precious time, telling us that we should reach nistha (and one time in Navadvipa you shocked us by saying ‘liberation’) before your departure from this world.

The first year I was with you, you were observing Guru Purnima in Mathura. I didn’t attend the ceremony in the morning because I was suffering from the heat and was covered with prickly heat rash. Instead, I went to honor you in the afternoon after the crowds had dispersed. I entered your room and you affectionately greeted me, asking, “Why you didn’t come this morning? Thousands of people came this morning, and with every person coming in front of me, I was wondering, ‘Where’s Vicitri? Where’s Vicitri? Why doesn’t she come?’” I was astonished that you noticed that I had not come in the morning. I’m such an insignificant person. How is it possible that you thought of me during such a busy festival with hundreds of devotees around you? Then you told me, “I am openly speaking about Vraja-bhakti and the love that Krsna shares with the gopis. In all the Gaudiya Mathas, they know about this Vraja-prema, but many of them will not tell others about it. And some of them criticize me for doing this. Now it is easily available, but only for the next 200 years. Then it will go underground. It will be very difficult to meet a rasika Vaisnava who can give it. Do you want it? If so, you must take advantage. If one doesn’t try for it now, the jiva may never have another chance.”

Many years later, at the end of your tour in the US one summer, you stopped overnight at Subala Sakha Prabhu’s house in New Jersey before flying to Holland on your way back to India. It was your intention to take full rest to prepare yourself for the long journey. As fate would have it, Pundarika Brahmacari’s relatives from North Carolina decided to come visit you; 6-8 adults and a few small kids piled into a large rented van and drove up to New Jersey to take advantage of your precious presence one last time. All of them stayed in the basement of Subala’s house. Needless to say, the house was bustling, with so many adults and the kids running up and down the stairs all day long. So what was the question of you having some quiet, private time? I observed how you gave up all thought for your own needs and selflessly gave yourself to your enthusiastic visitors. Some of them wanted initiation, one of them wanted a first-grains ceremony for her baby, and all of them simply wanted to chat with you. You kindly opened your door and graciously satisfied them. I was amazed to see this.

In mid-afternoon, around 3 or 4 o’clock, the house was quiet. Everyone was resting after initiations, fire yajna and prasad. So I took advantage of this quiet time and like a cat, stealthily went to your door, gently opened it and peeped inside. There you were, sitting peacefully, chanting. I went inside and with great admiration I said to you, “Gurudeva, you are vancha-kalpa-taru!” Smiling with dancing bright eyes and practically jumping out of your chair to embrace me, you answered with great enthusiasm: “Yes, and you are the root of that tree.” I was taken aback. “What? Don’t go overboard, Gurudeva. How can I be the root? I just want to be one little twig on that tree.” You reconsidered and realized that perhaps you had gone a little too far. You thought for a moment and then proffered a more reasonable reply. “Yes! You are the fragrance of that tree.” Such a perfect, beautiful analogy you gave, seeing me in my pure Vraja-svarupa. To be the recipient of your deep affection and to bathe in the beauty of your open heart in this way was a special event for me. I could never have imagined the love and the acceptance that you showered on me that day. As I relate this story now, I am feeling that joy still alive and growing.

“Follow the process. Meditate on one verse at a time, and mantra-mayi upasana will come. One lila will manifest. Eventually all lilas will come together one after another in your mind and you will be able to do bhajana. The astonishing bhava of your guru will manifest in you also. Don’t hanker for position. Mercy of Krsna will come if one is doing sadhana. No need to do anything else. An ekantika bhakta (one-pointed devotee) will get fully absorbed in bhajana, but will still follow all vidhi. Just like Radha in separation, She will embrace a tamal tree and think it is Krsna, but She will never embrace Uddhava, who looks just like Krsna, because She will not transgress the rules of prema. Following this process, we will easily cross over the samsara.”

“Remember phal-vikreni, the fruit seller from Mathura. She made up her mind that today she would not leave without seeing Krsna. The sadhaka must have that same resolve. ‘Day and night I will practice bhakti as Mahaprabhu and Rupa Gosvami instructed. With love I will chant a fixed number of Hari-nama and with my whole energy serve Hari-Guru-Vaisnavas. Every day I will study Srimad-Bhagavatam and our Gosvami granthas. I will not waste a minute. Every day I will recite Gopi Gita, Venu Gita, Bhramara Gita. If I meet a rasika Vaisnava, I will dedicate my whole life to serving him. I will visit the places of Radha-Krsna’s secret lilas and piteously pray and recite poems full of longing. Then, by the mercy of sad-guru, Sri Yugala-Kisora will surely give me Their darsana.’ This is the process.”

I will dust myself with the kunkuma of your words. Let me dwell in the protective, all-encompassing cocoon of your heart:

1. your instructions
2. your katha
3. your smiles
4. your tender glances
5. your sarcastic chastisements

Vicitri Dasi
