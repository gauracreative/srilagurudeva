Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Vasanti dasi

India, Vrindavan

I offer my humble obeisances at the lotus feet of nitya-lila pravista om visnupada astottara-sata sri rupanuga acarya varya Sri Srimad Bhaktivedanta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja.

My dear Srila Gurudeva,

It's your centennial and 10 years since you left the vision of this material world. For your centennial, we are all collectively writing something to you, which is hard for many of us to do. Yet with the sense of duty to do so, it has created a sense of unity around you, as you are the focal point of our lives. So, my heart is black and my feelings numb, but my only solace is in prayer to you, having faith that you are my paramatma and simultaneously hear and reply.

There is a piece in us all that feels a need to hide from the world, at least for me. A part that is embarrassed by our existence and all our karma from so many lives that we have brought with us to this human form. We know intellectually that we are baddha-jivas and now have this rare human form of life to use in the service of Hari, Guru and Vaisnavas, but our emotional side has a tendency to feel unworthy and hopeless about our existence. Yet relief comes in the form of Sri Guru – as you yourself wrote perfectly in your bhajan Sri Guru-Carana-Kamala Bhaja Mana: “This rare human birth is not often found as we scour the fourteen planes of existence. A chance few, by astounding good fortune, get to glimpse a devotee of Śrī Hari.” Astounding good fortune...how I attained this astounding good fortune, I do not know. Yet this astounding good fortune allowed me a glimpse of you, and by this glimpse I feel a ray of hope, a sense that this embarrassment of my existence is unwarranted because my existence is now worthwhile having received news from the spiritual realm of the goal and true purpose of life; that the hole I want to dig at times to hide my innumerable bad qualities has been blocked up by a reservoir of love and mercy in you, which makes me feel human and hopeful that developing love for Sri Sri Radha Govinda is attainable in a future lifetime.

I don’t know Krsna, I don’t know Radharani, I don’t know Mahaprabhu, I don’t even know you, but by some divine mercy, you came to this world and poured your boundless mercy on the entire world. Krsna is bhakta-vatsala – so loving to His devotees - even to an elephant like Gajendra. When he began drowning, he called for the Supreme Lord, “Oh Lord, please help me,” so the Lord went as fast as he could on Garuda and jumped off Garuda for His devotee. Similarly, your being a manifestation of Krsna (saksad-dharitvena) is bhakti-vatsala.

Your love for all living entities is without bounds – incomprehensible, actually. I remember when we were in Malalysia and you heard the news that Puru Prabhu left this world, you announced in class with tears in your eyes that your dear friend Puru had left this world.

Once when we were in Toronto you said you always wanted to see Niagra Falls. The few of us that were there got to go along. I had been getting sicker and sicker all along the tour from changing climates. Toronto was freezing and Niagra Falls was even more cold. When we returned, you and your sevakas went to rest, so I asked Ananga Didi whose house we were at if she could give me a treatment as she is an acupuncturist. She suggested I lay down on the deck in the sun, and she put needles in my stomach and chest. I was praying to you in my mind about my health – that if it was your desire, that I get well for the remainder of the tour. A few minutes later, Syamarani Didi who was sitting on a chair, chanting, jumped up and said, “Oh, Gurudeva.” I opened my eyes, and there you were, standing at the door. You looked at us and then went back in the house. I felt so embarrassed. I stood up, not really knowing what to do as I had needles in me. I stood by the door and we saw you standing in the living room. Syamarani Didi asked you if you needed anything, and you didn’t reply, and then you walked out of the house and stood outside.

Then, Ananga Priya Didi's husband, Sasvata Prabhu, went outside to meet you, and you told him that you went outside for some sun as the room was cold. (I was lying in the spot where you usually sat). I told Vrnda Didi I felt so bad that I was in Gurudeva’s spot, and she said not to worry about it. Being the mental person that I am, though, I felt devastated. A couple of hours later, Vrnda Didi was about to leave for the airport to go to the next city (as she always went to the city a day ahead of Gurudeva to prepare for his arrival). She was about to go say goodbye to Gurudeva and asked me if I wanted to come, which I did. I said to him, “Gurudeva, I’m sorry I was in your spot in the sun. I have a bad cough and Ananga was giving me acupuncture.” He said, “Why are you sorry? I am sorry. You are my daughter, I am your father, I am worried for you. I do not want you to be sick.”

If this isn’t bhakta-vatsala, I don’t know what is.

Krsna is also vadanya – merciful. He reciprocates with His devotees. He says in the Gita, "As those who surrender to Me, I will reciprocate." He mercifully gave Sudama brahmana and his wife a palace in return for their seva. And Mahaprabhu is maha-vadanyaya because He is Krsna who came to this world with the mood and complexion of Srimati Radharani to distribute ujjvala-prema.

Yet Gurudeva is maha maha-vadanya – as he is the transcendental associate of Sri Sri Radha-Govinda and Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu and has come to pick up all the jivas from the filth of maya and distribute the same ujjvala-prema.

Thank you for coming to the West, especially to Los Angeles to find me.

Your daughter and aspiring servant,
Vasanti dasi
