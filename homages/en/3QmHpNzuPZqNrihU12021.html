Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Mohini devi dasi

Costa Rica, San José

Srila Gurudeva during the wedding ceremony. Sri Navadwipa-dhama, Koladwipa, Amalaki Ekadasi, 17 March 2008.

Jay Sri Sri Guru Gauranga! Jay Sri Sri Radha Vinoda Bihariji!

I offer my humble and respectful dandavat-pranamas to the lotus feet of my dear Srila Gurudeva, nitya-lila pravista om visnupada paramahamsa, astottara-sata, Sri Srimad Bhaktivedanta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja. I also offer my heartfelt dandavat-pranama to Srila Bhakti Pramoda Puri Gosvami Maharaja, Srila Bhaktivedanta Swami Maharaja and to all of our guru-varga.

Although I feel embarrassed to present a puspanjali at the lotus feet of Srila Gurudeva that will be made public, nevertheless my debt of gratitude to him, as well as the guidance of my senior godbrothers and godsisters, moves me to write these words. Due to my lack of qualification, I will refer to my memories of Srila Gurudeva, with the hope that the world will see that he touched the lives of so many, and his teachings continue to guide us through these challenging times we live in.

Srila Gurudeva mercifully gave me initiation by phone in July 2002. This was arranged by Srimati Radhasundari didi and Sripad Ramesh prabhu, as well as Sripad Bhaktivedanta Damodara Maharaja, who was then Radhanath Brahmacari. I was 21-years old at that time, and I had never met in person he who would become my Gurudeva. I had just gone through the difficult experience of having taken harinama from a Bengali sannyasi whose conduct became reproachable and who soon after left Costa Rica. Both Srila Gurudeva and his disciples began by explaining to me guru-tattva and particularly the true qualification of a spiritual master. He or she is a person who is detached from mundane matters, who has full knowledge of siddhanta and can answer all questions, and who has realization of the Supreme Truth. I also came to know the qualification of a genuine disciple: a person who, after understanding that this world has nothing to offer, surrenders at the lotus feet of a genuine guru, humbly inquiries from him or her, and who serves this guru with sincerity in order to attain the highest goal of life.

tasmād guruṁ prapadyeta
jijñāsuḥ śreya uttamam
śābde pare ca niṣṇātaṁ
brahmaṇy upaśamāśrayam
–(Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, 11.3.21)

I came to realize I had none of that detachment, sincerity or humility that is required of a sad-sisya.

In 2003, I met Srila Gurudeva in Alachua, Florida, where his vyasa-puja was celebrated. Srila Gurudeva was 82 years-old at that time. In that same year, he mercifully gave me diksa initiation by phone, again, through the generous mediation of the same dear devotees who had facilitated my harinama initiation. In 2004, I finally traveled to Vrndavan dhama, India, with my mother. Srila Gurudeva that year decided to conduct Sri Navadwipa dhama parikrama in Vraja, so he mercifully gave me harinama and diksa initiation once more on the day of Sri Gaura Purnima, in Sri Giridhari Gaudiya Math in Sri Govardhana, a temple that was still under construction.

During all these years, I would write to Srila Gurudeva by e-mail through his secretary, Sripad Brajanath prabhu, who would kindly convey my silly letters to Srila Gurudeva. He patiently instructed me to chant harinama, study sastra, particularly Jaiva-dharma, Bhagavad-gita, Sri Sikshastakam, Sri Upadesamrta and Manah-siksa, do service for his Spanish publications, and share his instructions, mainly by giving a good example to others through ideal personal conduct. He also instructed me to be tolerant and never criticise or give pain to anyone, and to gradually engage all of my time in devotional activities. I would try to go and see him once a year in different parts of the world, in one way or other.

I was born in a family of academics, and I was very focused at the time on my university studies in Law and Politics. Srila Gurudeva told me he would always protect me and guide me while I was doing my studies, and, at the same time, he also encouraged me to cultivate a strong sadhana. Even in 2010, when I saw him last in Italy, he would still ask me when I would do a PhD. Srila Gurudeva always treated equally his female and male disciples, and never gave women any indication that there was anything we could not do, in terms of our life in society or our spiritual life. He wanted his female disciples to be qualified and responsible in all ways, to be independent and to speak boldly. He set an Australian disciple of his, Damodara-priya Dasi, as an example and told me I should be like her: the best Law student, a serious sadhaka and a bold speaker. He also told me, however, that if I really wanted to help others (at that time I wanted to become a human rights lawyer), I could only do so if I became advanced and strong in Krsna consciousness.

Srila Gurudeva giving instructions during the wedding ceremony.

While Gurudeva never encouraged anyone to marry, as he would focus on teaching bhakti-yoga, and he would never meddle in his disciples’ personal affairs, in 2008 he kindly agreed to marry me to Sundar Gopal Das, his dear disciple, on the day of Amalaki Ekadasi in Sri Navadwipa dhama. In the ceremony, Gurudeva instructed us to always be friends and to support each other in sadhana and service to Sri Guru and Vaisnavas. I feel happy and protected in this grhasta-ashrama, which Srila Bhaktivinoda Thakura and our Gurudeva have explained, can be a place for spiritual growth, provided such growth is our priority.

In total, I spent fifteen years studying. When all these studies turned out to be a massive disappointment—nothing in my studies could help me overcome illusion, ignorance, fear, confusion, lamentation or death, and rather, they were simply a source of suffering—Gurudeva’s instructions held me from what would have been a massive existential crisis. And while still very much a conditioned soul, I understood that Gurudeva had been the only person who had ever told me the truth about this world, and who had selflessly motivated me to question who I am, why I am here, and what my purpose is.

Srila Gurudeva continues to be present through his disciples who guide me, especially Srimati Uma Didi, through his books, videos and recordings of his lectures, through our Deities of Sri Sri Gaura Nitai he mercifully installed and the salagrama-sila he allowed my husband and I to worship, and, of course, through the mantras he gave us. He has provided us with everything. He has never left us, and he has never forsaken us. My only prayer is to one day follow his instructions fully and properly and thus be able to understand who he really is and remember him at the time of my death. I hope one day to be the sincere disciple that a guru of his calibre deserves and to follow him to where he resides eternally.

Aspiring for the service of Sri Guru and Vaisnavas,

Mohini Devi Dasi.
