Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Tarunkrsna das

Australia, Murwillumbah

vande ham sri-guroh sri-yuta-pada-kamalam sri-gurum vaisnavams ca
sri-rupam sagrajatam saha-guna-raghunathanvitam tam sa-jivam
sadvaitam savadhutam parijana-sahitam krsna-caitanya-devam
sri-radha-krsna-padan saha-gana-lalita-sri-vishakanvitams ca

guruve gauracandraya radhikayai tadalaye
krsnaya krsna-bhaktaya tad-bhaktaya namo namah

om ajnana-timirandhasya jnananjana-salakaya
caksur unmilitam yena tasmai sri-guruve namah

namah om visnu-padaya radhikaya-priyatmane
sri-srimad-bhakti-vedanta narayana iti namine

My dear Srila Gurudeva,

It has been said that upon the entrance to this hellish material world there is a sign reading, “Abandon hope all who enter here!”

That sign is correct! It is not a mistake, for this dark, uncaring realm of self-centered materialism is the epitome of hopelessness, for when we associate with darkness, we, in our own darkness, become even darker still, again and again ad infinitum.

Yet, there is hope here, even for one such as myself, degraded as I am, even as a lost soul immersed in deluded selfishness: You, my sweet-natured Gurudeva, you are that hope. Sri Guru is the only hope within this world. Sri Guru, along with sadhu-sanga and Krsna-katha, is the enemy of our ignorance.

Srila Gurudeva, you bring joy where previously there was none, you bring love to the unloving; you bring hope of the highest magnitude to the suffering souls. You give us instructions that can erase our ignorance and you, undemanding as you are, do not abandon us even as we, in our partial surrender, ignore many, if not most of those very instructions: you are kind hearted with a personal touch in every communication, forgiving and patient, even towards a wretch such as myself. There is no possible way that I could ever repay you, but fortunately, eternity permits me a chance to endeavor in actions that may, hopefully, bring some pleasure to your heart of hearts.

Your kindness knows no bounds. Time and time again you shower your mercy upon me, despite my confused mind indulging in prajalpa, pride, and sense gratification time and time again; two steps forward, 1,999 steps backward – rinse and repeat. And yet, somehow, your unfailing compassion keeps me holding on to the holy lotus feet of Lord Nityananda.

Whilst ever your mercy flows, there is hope.

I wonder when I will become a big hearted Vaisnava akin to yourself, for surely that is required if I am ever to bring happiness to your wonderful self and to Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu. When will kindness become my middle name?

Srila Gurudeva, you come here, under no compulsion, lusting for no material end, desiring only to free the suffering souls of this miasma of dark things, wherein the unforgiving inhabitants compete for pride of place, erroneously blaming each other for their unhappiness; unaware of the beautiful reality of Sri Sri Radha Krsna awaiting us beyond ignorance, pride and selfishness.

You come only to save us from our misery.

The above is obvious to all who know you to any small extent. That begs the question of how do I glorify you, abundantly and in all reality? I fear that this is beyond me. I have so little actual comprehension of how far above me you stand.

Yes, I can pretend to glorify you, insincere prattle, artificial platitudes, meaningless melodrama, empty and hollow, merely so that I may bask in the radiant glow of your perfection. But if imitation be the most sincere form of flattery, it behooves me to make an effort, however feeble, however unenlightened, for despite being the very least of your disciples, you still shower your mercy upon me such that life becomes abundantly littered with realization after realization. Not regarding greatness so much, unfortunate as that may be, but much of the time, of my fallen, useless condition, my imperfections, the crying shame of my insignificance. Perhaps now, by contrast, some understanding, some inkling, some modicum of a realization regarding the greatness of Sri Guru may dawn upon me.

Oh my dear Srila Gurudeva, please, on this extra special, uber-auspicious occasion of your 100th anniversary, I pray that you shower your mercy upon all of us who languish here, viciously feasting upon our false egos, imagining ourselves as pinnacles of success, when in reality, we are tiny spirit souls, not far removed from nothing, barely conscious of ourselves (if that), impotent to the nth degree, utterly dependent upon a higher power yet wishfully thinking that we are great, critical of others, drowning whilst clutching at the phantasmagorical straws of an imagined superiority, all the while foolishly enslaved to maya. Srila Gurudeva, we are always in need of your mercy because, no, we fallen spirit souls are not great.

It is you, Srila Gurudeva, abundantly qualified to instruct the entire world, blessed with a magnanimous, caring nature, perfectly fit to associate with Sri Sri Radha Krsna whilst engaged in very intimate sweet pastimes with Their Lordships and intimate associates. It is you, Srila Gurudeva who are a great personality.

Gurudeva, YOU are glorious!

Srila Gurudeva ki jaya!

Aspiring for your service,
Tarunkrsna Das
