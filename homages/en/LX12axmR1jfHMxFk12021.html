Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Madhuri Devi Dasi

United States, Rhode Island

I offer my humble obeisances at the lotus feet of my beloved Gurudeva, Sri Srimad Bhaktivedanta Narayana Goswami Maharaja.

Dearest, beloved Gurudeva

You are a thief. You have stolen my heart. Absolutely, and I have no recourse, nor do I wish any.

You are my heart, my life, the hope of my soul. You are the water of eternality that slakes the burning dryness of my very being. You are my most beloved and the essence of all that I long for.

I don’t have the eyes to see you or the intelligence to know you or to extol your glories. I am just starting out, and you are blessedly engaging as mother, as I take my first baby steps towards becoming a devotee. You are giving me the love and the guidance that lifts me from this fallen place. And I fight you every step of the way, yet you never abandon me.

I know you want the very best for me, and suffer at the foolishness and obstinacy and the wayward wandering of this misguided and derelict child, responding in never-ending mercy with a mother’s love. Perhaps this is why I was denied a mother in this lifetime, that I might come to know you as my only true well wisher.

Gurudeva, when I was little, my heart’s desire was to understand how everything in this fascinating and crazy mixed up world comes together to weave this tapestry of life in all of its variegated beauty. I sat with my father and brothers at the dinner table and read from dictionaries and encyclopedias. I studied biology and chemistry and comparative religions. I delighted in the calculations and clarity, misleading in their simplicity, of mathematics and the precision of metals and engineering.

I became an inventor, a problem solver, a creator of things of beauty and utility. All ultimately dry and without taste, lacking the essence of divinity and service. Meaningless, and so I settled on learning about people, by far the most fascinating of all. Or so I believed. What a fool’s errand, to look at others, to look to others for knowledge when one does not know one’s self.

om ajñāna-timirāndhasya jñānāñjana-śalākayā
caksur unmīlitam yena tasmai śrī gurave namah

Beloved Gurudeva, you are so merciful. I offer you my humble obeisances, my heart a million times in the dust of your lotus feet, and beg for your mercy, heart and soul, that with the torchlight of divine knowledge you will open my eyes; my eyes have been blinded throughout innumerable lifetimes with this darkness of ignorance.

Joni Mitchell wrote a song that was a mnemonic in my head throughout childhood, that sings boldly and clearly now. “Don't it always seem to go, that you don't know what you've got till it's gone. They paved paradise, put up a parking lot.” Gurudeva, I miss you terribly, even though I feel you daily as you reside in my heart. I miss your physical presence, your ability to look right through me, your willingness to bring succor to this insignificant soul. While your true being is a total expression of your absolute and complete, unequivocal surrender to the service of your beloved swamini, Srimati Radhika.

Gurudeva, why don’t I have the eyes to see you and the heart to know you? I’ve bumbled through this lifetime with a vague understanding that my soul is only enlivened and satisfied through service. That must be sukriti, your mercy, lifetime after lifetime, as I could never have come to this truth without unlimited lifetimes of stumbling. It is such a rare gift, eternities in the making, and part of that vague awareness is that you have been sitting in my heart, oh so patiently, gradually eroding away my arrogance and confusion, my raging and blaming, to uncover the seed of my soul and true nature. It is so deeply painful, this knowledge, as my human nature and conditioning makes every action of mine an act of contrariness and offense. And without your physical presence, your direct association, it is so much harder to cross this ocean of nescience. So many tough lessons. So much to atone for. And yet so much to reach for and embrace. So much light you shine, and with it, the most exquisite of blessings.

Gurudeva, please please, I beg of you, make me worthy of serving you. Instill in me willingness, the greed that is essential to navigate this single truth, this soul truth and absolute reality. Guide me. Make me worthy to be the servant of the servant of your servant, and ultimately, train me to have the qualification to become a maidservant of your Beloved, the most merciful Srimati Radharani.

You play with me and tease me. I understand you are so very very deeply serious about instructions to me; my very soul depends on your mercy. With your divine knowledge, you know just the alchemy I need and you mete it out in perfect measure. Yes, in this raw stage of my nascent development I require both the stick and the carrot, as holding me on the path of awakening requires a firm hand and I haven’t the discernment to make the choices that point me towards devotion and service, my rightful calling. And yet, despite my obstinacy, you guide me with tenderness and humor and the long vision begot of eternal knowledge. I need your humor and your teasing. They give me hope, and make light of this material suffering; they help me to see the farcical nature of arrogance and false ego, and deliver brief flashes of clarity about the temporary nature of this material experience, and the absurd, albeit painful, tangle of miseries I dwell in, entirely of my own misbegotten crafting.

Your laughter makes light of what is ultimately insignificant, and lights up your eyes with such love. When I remember you, my heart bursts with that love. I am so beyond blessed that you have come for me, in all of my foolishness and mean and blindsided temperamental humanness. You bring to light what is covered. You have given me my heart, and blessed it with the seed of bhakti. And you are constantly tending it, even as I am reluctant or unable, in my willful independence and blindness. You give me unlimited benediction!

Blessings beyond blessings! Surpassing all I could ever have imagined! Harikatha is my lifeblood. I could never have imagined that to listen is to serve. Yet just by hearing of your glories, the glories of the Vaisnavas, and the pastimes of Vrajendra Nandana Shyamasundhara in the kunjas of Braj, the unparalleled mercy of our most beloved Srimati Radhika, the lilas and teachings of Sriman Mahaprabhu, what is inauspicious and temporary is lessening its hold on me, and I am beginning, slowly slowly, to be attracted. There could be no greater blessing in this lifetime, than to be awakened to the truth and beauty and eternality of divine service, and one’s nitya dharma as an eternal servitor of the divine personality of Godhead, Sri Sri Radha Krsna.

Daily I am learning to see, to notice. And you are so present, working on me, sometimes subtly and sometimes with raw and so so very merciful force, the force of your love, tuning my heart, senses and intellect to see and serve. I am noticing. So often now my heart calls forth the lilas, and I feel the indifference in my heart slowly slowly moving towards affection and remembrance. You are giving me not the templates to pattern from, but the absolute truth. Your heart and presence and alchemy are little by little sparking the slightest taste and knowledge in me of the absolute reality of loving relationship, and are carrying me through the heart of how this soul is destined, through your guidance, to take on the true mantle of love.

You are fulfilling my conscious prayer:

“Teach me to be a loving soul. Take me and use me as a vessel for love, compassion and service, and teach me devotion, gratitude and humility.”

You have taken this prayer and are not only fulfilling it, you are showing me so so much more. You are introducing me to the unlimited nature of bhakti, that I may accept that my true nature is eternal, and my only true dharma is to serve.

*sraddha, sambandha, saranagati*

Dearmost beloved Gurudeva, I beg of you to teach me surrender, that I may lay my heart at your lotus feet. Instill in me that greed that is never satisfied. May I weep for all eternity for your association. Dear dear beloved Gurudeva, you are mine. Allow me to always keep you close, here, in my heart.

Aspiring to serve the servants of the servants of the servants of you, my most beloved Gurudeva, that one day I may serve you, and through your divine mercy, be called to the service of your beloved swamini, Srimati Radhika.

Madhuri Devi dasi

Providence, Rhode Island, USA
