Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Lalita Devi Dasi

India, New Delhi

nama om visnu-padaya radhikayai-priyatmane
sri-srimad bhaktvedanta-narayana iti namine
gurave gauracandraya radhikayai tadalaye
krsnaya krsna-bhaktaya tad-bhaktaya namo namah

I still wonder and revel in the bliss thinking how my gurudeva, an ocean of mercy that he is, gave me a place in his lotus feet and is my ETERNAL spiritual master, although I am most fallen.

All this feels like a dream now and I want to go on living in this dream till I meet him. When Gurudeva was doing his manifest pastimes, everyday was like a FESTIVAL! With Gurudeva we were always in Vraja.

Gurudeva would talk about how Sri Radha Ji serves Krsna in various beautiful ways and is waiting for us to join her yutha, in Sri Lalita Sakhi's gana and under the guidance of Sri Rupa and Rati Manjari and our gurudeva (Sri Ramana Manjari) and our guru-parampara who are all dedicated, innocent palya-dasis of Sri Radha Ji.

Gurudeva would say, “What is our relationship with Balarama Ji and then would lovingly describe Dau Bhaiya's various Vraja pastimes, also how he is the akhanda-guru-tattva, the presiding deity of sandhini-sakti, and how our very own existence depends on him.

Gurudeva would speak nectarean harikatha from the scriptures, tying us to Vraja and immersing us in the ocean of madhurya-rasa. I am fortunate to have got his association from 1995 to 2010 and during this time life was a SPIRITUAL WHIRLWIND revolving around Gurudeva.

Gurudeva used to very often come to Delhi where I stayed and also to NCR. He would come in many house programs and his disciples were increasing everyday.

At one such house programme I remember I sang the following bhajan:

hari hari kabe mora habe hena dina
vimala vaisnave rati upajibe vasana haibe ksina
–From the song "Hari Hari Kabe Mora Habe" by Srila Bhaktivinoda Thakura

The bhajan made him so happy, and he was so kind to especially come up to me and bless me.

I remember once in Mathura some devotees were recording his lecture in the evening class. As he got up to leave after the class was over, he was little annoyed and said, “What do you all keep doing with this recording-recording? We kept our guru in our heart. Recording will not do....Keep guru in your heart.”

I also remember how Once at Adi-Badrinatha during Vraja-mandala parikrama, somehow my daughter and I were amongst the first ones to reach up to where Gurudeva was sitting with his back towards Tapta-Kunda.

He looked at me and very loudly said, “I have taken you out from swamps....I will not leave you....Even if I have to go to Naraka-loka and pull you out from there."

His words were so much like the cool waters of Tapta-Kunda upon our burning lives in this material world. I realize the depth of the words spoken by Gurudeva more and more now with every passing year when he is not manifest to the eyes.

Gurudeva did not belong to this material world. I would often feel how unfortunate I am that I am not able to stay in Vraja all the time and attend his divine harikatha. But see how merciful Gurudeva is!

He arranged for all his lectures to be restored, bringing in really special people for this seva too, and against all odds made all those words he spoke way back in 1990s available to all of us again.

As Syamarani Didi also put it, “Gurudeva has come a second time for all of us."

And in all those lectures he is reminding us incessantly, “Don’t pray for any other thing except Sri Radha-Dasyam.”

The pain of separation from Gurudeva is mitigated by hearing his words again and again and reliving those cherished moments.

I pray to my gurudeva to give me strength to keep going on - on the path shown by him and never to let me go any where away from his lotus feet.

SRILA GURUDEV KI JAYA!

Lalita Devi Dasi

?? I am Lalita Devi Dasi, a diksa disciple of Srila Bhaktivedanta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja. He gave me diksa in February 1996. I am from Karol Bagh, New Delhi.
