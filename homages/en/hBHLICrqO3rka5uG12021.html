United States, Las Vegas, Nevada

Srila Gurudeva Offering Flowers to Thakurji on the Altar in Our Home

Śrῑ Śrῑ Guru-Gaurāṅgau Jayataḥ.

All glories to Śrῑ Guru and Śrῑ Gaurāṅga. All glories to Śrῑ Śrῑ Rādhā Krṣṇa Nitya Yugal.

On the occasion of our beloved Gurudeva Nityalῑlā Praviṣṭa Oṁ Viṣṇupād Śrῑ Śrῑmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyaṇa Gosvāmῑ Mahārāja’s appearance day, I intend to write a few paragraphs on His glorious lῑlāmrita (i.e., nectarian past times). I wish to glorify my paramārādhya (i.e., supremely worshipable) Gurudeva but, unfortunately, I don’t have proper qualification to do so. I have no pānditya (i.e., scholarship), no vairāgya (i.e., detachment from Māyā), nor I have even a smell of bhakti (i.e., devotion). Then, how can I glorify such a great Vaiṣṇava as Śrῑla Gurudeva? Our śāstras (i.e., revealed scriptures) teach us that Śrῑ Guru is like a kalpa taru (i.e., desire tree). Also, He is the greatest well wisher of His disciples. Śrῑ Guru’s āśῑrvāda (i.e., blessing) can fulfill the disciple’s all desires. Therefore, I humbly pray to Śrῑla Gurudeva, saying, O Śrῑla Gurudeva, you are an ocean of mercy (Śrῑ Guru karunā sindhu adhama janār bandhu), kindly bestow your āśῑrvāda on me so that I can put together in writing a good number sentences to glorify you on this very special day of your appearance in this world.

Śrῑla Gurudeva appeared in a village named Tewaripārā in the Indian state of Bihār in 1921 to become a savior of fallen souls (i.e., patita pāvana) later on. He lived in this world for a period of approximately 90 years. During the first 25 years of his life, He was a householder and followed grihastha dharma. But his life changed drastically when He met Nityalῑlā Praviṣṭa Oṁ Viṣṇupād Śrῑ Śrῑmad Bhakti Prajñāna Keśava Gosvāmῑ Mahārāja in 1946. Soon after this meeting, He gave up his family life and joined Śrῑla Keśava Gosvāmῑ Mahārāja’s Matha. After joining His Gurudeva’s Matha, He sincerely practiced and preached Gauḍῑya Vaiṣṇava culture in India and wrote many books in Hindi, His native language. He gave His heart to His Gurudeva and totally engaged His body and mind to accomplish His Gurudeva’s mission of preaching Gauḍῑya Vaiṣṇava philosophy and culture (i.e., Krṣṇa Consciousness). Through His dedicated Gurusevā, very soon He established Himself as a perfect Gurudāsa (i.e., servant of Śrῑ Guru). He continuously served His Gurudeva with utmost sincerity till 1968, when His Guru Mahārāja entered Nitya Lῑlā. After His Gurudeva’s disappearance, He continued to work together with His God brothers, Nityalῑlā Praviṣṭa Oṁ Viṣṇupād Śrῑla Bhaktivedānta Vāmana Gosvāmῑ Mahārāja and Nityalῑlā Praviṣṭa Oṁ Viṣṇupād Śrῑla Bhaktivedānta Trivikrama Gosvāmῑ Mahārāja, for further realization of Their Gurudeva’s desire to preach Krṣṇa Consciousness everywhere, following the footsteps of Śrῑ Caitanya Mahāprabhu and His divine associates.

Historically, Śrῑ Caitanya Mahāprabhu, who is Śrῑ Krṣṇa Himself, initiated the Krṣṇa Consciousness movement in India more than 500 years ago and envisaged that one day His movement will reach each town and every village on earth (prithivῑte āse yata nagarādi grām, sarvatra pracār haibe mora nām). To fulfill Śrῑ Caitanya Mahāprabhu’s desire, Śrῑla Bhakti Prajñāna Keśava Gosvāmῑ Mahārāja started preaching Krṣṇa Consciousness throughout India. His younger God brother Nityalῑlā Praviṣṭa Oṁ Viṣṇupād Śrῑla A. C. Bhaktivedānta Svāmῑ Mahārāja Prabhupāda came to America in 1965 to preach Krṣṇa Consciousness in the western world. Śrῑla Prabhupāda, the Founder Acārya of the International Society for Krṣṇa Consciousness, achieved many successes in His endeavors and established Rādhā Krṣṇa Temples in almost all major cities in the world. Śrῑla Gurudeva helped Śrῑla Prabhupāda by sending numerous things Śrῑla Prabhupāda needed for preaching Krṣṇa Consciousness in America. Also, when Śrῑla Prabhupāda entered Nitya Lῑlā in 1977, per His previous request, Śrῑla Gurudeva placed Śrῑla Prabhupāda’s body in samādhi. More than a decade after Śrῑla Prabhupāda’s disappearance, Śrῑla Gurudeva decided to preach Krṣṇa consciousness in the western world and came to America in 1996.

Śrῑla Gurudeva’s appearance in India and His subsequent preaching of Krṣṇa Consciousness in the western world have been very auspicious. In the west, thousands of fallen people were eagerly waiting for spiritual guidance from a Uttam Adhikari Vaiṣṇava like Śrῑla Gurudeva. A large number of new people took initiation from Him. Lots of Śrῑla Prabhupāda’s followers found their Prabhupāda in Śrῑla Bhaktivedānta Nārāyaṇa Gosvāmῑ Mahārāja and took shelter at His lotus feet for spiritual advancement. Śrῑla Gurudeva’s preaching of Krṣṇa Consciousness in the west has been particularly beneficial to my family and me. Although I was totally unqualified, and perhaps I was one of the most fallen persons in the three worlds, Śrῑla Gurudeva did not have any hesitation to give my family and me shelter at His lotus feet. Such was His greatness! Śrῑla Gurudeva accepted us as His disciples during our very first meeting in 1998 in Badger, California. In this context, I express my sincere gratitude and thankfulness to Śrῑpād Upānanda Prabhu (presently Śrῑpād Avadhuta Mahārāja) and Śrῑpād Mithilādhῑśa Prabhu for introducing my family and me to Śrῑla Gurudeva.

After receiving Śrῑla Gurudeva’s mercy through initiation, we attended His programs in several American cities and listened sweet Harikathā from His lotus mouth. But that was too little in terms of our Gurusevā; so, we developed an intense desire to personally serve Him. For this reason, I kept inviting Śrῑla Gurudeva and His closest associates (i.e., Śrῑpād Mādhava Mahārāja and Śrῑpād Brajanāth Prabhu) to visit our house in Las Vegas, Nevada. Finally, Śrῑpād Mādhava Mahārāja and Śrῑpād Brajanāth Prabhu accepted my invitation in 2007 and accompanied Śrῑla Gurudeva during His first visit to our house in that year. Śrῑla Gurudeva, along with His closest associates, visited us once a year for four consecutive years from 2007 to 2010. Our happiness and joyfulness knew no bounds when Śrῑla Gurudeva’s lotus feet touched the floor of our house.

We were extremely happy because His visit gave us a special opportunity to serve Him personally. I was so happy for the opportunity to bring Śrῑla Gurudeva and His closest associates from the airport to our house. When He was in our house, I had the opportunity to wash His lotus feet with water and drink bhakta pada jal, place a flower garland around His neck every morning, take Him out for morning walks in a nearby park, massage His lotus feet at noon and in the evening, wash His clothes and dry them in the sun, make humble enquiries about important siddhāntas (i.e., conclusions) of our śāstras, request him to remove our doubts regarding some difficult-to-understand siddhāntas, and to organize devotional gatherings in our house as well as in the local Hindu temple, where Śrῑla Gurudeva spoke sweet Harikathā to invited devotees. Also, every morning we joyfully watched Śrῑla Gurudeva offering flowers to deities in our temple room. Moreover, my wife liked the opportunity to cook for Śrῑla Gurudeva under the supervision of Śrῑpād Mādhava Mahārāja. Our daughters brought flowers from the market and made flower garlands for Śrῑla Gurudeva. In addition, we all had the good fortune of honoring Śrῑla Gurudeva’s Mahā Mahāprasadam three times per day when He was with us in Las Vegas. Śrῑpād Mādhava Mahārāja, Śrῑpād Brajanath Prabhu, and I used to make a lot of jokes while eating snacks together. All of these precious (i.e., auspicious) moments of our association with Śrῑla Gurudeva and His closest associates will remain fresh in our memory forever.

Śrῑla Gurudeva came to the West at a very advanced age. But His old age could not deter Him from extensively preaching Krṣṇa Consciousness throughout the world. He continued to preach Krṣṇa Consciousness all over the world until late 2010, when he received a call from His Svāmῑni Śrῑmati Rādhārāni to go back to Goloka-Vrndāvana to render service as Her maid servant (i.e., as Her pālya dāsῑ or manjari). Śrῑla Gurudeva happily accepted the offer and disappeared in no time. It is important to note that the appearance and disappearance of Vaiṣṇavas like Śrῑla Gurudeva are very different from the birth and death of ordinary people. Common people are bound by the results of their karma. By contrast, Vaiṣṇavas are not bound by any karma (na karma bandhanaṁ janma Vaiṣṇavānaṁ ca vidyate). Vaiṣṇavas like Śrῑla Gurudeva appear in this world and disappear from it at their own will.

When Śrῑla Gurudeva disappeared, He left with us a treasury of spiritual wealth that consists of memories of auspicious moments of our association with Him, His vivid example of perfect Gurusevā, several dozens of scholarly books He authored, and innumerable audio and video recordings of His sweet Harikathā. This invaluable spiritual wealth will guide us to become true Gurudāsa, perform devotional service in the mode of pure goodness, and attain Krṣṇa prema (i.e., intense love for Krṣṇa).

Continued remembrance of the auspicious memories of our association with Śrῑla Gurudeva will give us a feeling of separation from Him (i.e., viraha or vipralambha) and draw us closer to Him. Going closer to a bonafide sannyāsῑ like Śrῑla Gurudeva will enhance our vairāgya (i.e., detachment from Māyā). This will gradually but eventually place us in the mode of pure goodness, which, in turn, will qualify us to perform pure bhakti. Then, we will be able to render pure devotional service to Śrῑ Śrῑ Rādhā Krṣṇa Nitya Yugal. The result of pure Krṣṇa bhakti is Krṣṇa prema, which will bring about an end to our material existence (bhakti phal prema haya, saṅsār jāya kṣaya) and take us in Śrῑ Krṣṇa’s eternal abode to perpetually render loving service to Śrῑ Śrῑ Rādhā Krṣṇa Nitya Yugal.

Śrῑla Gurudeva’s prakata (i.e., manifested) lῑlā in this world is a lesson on perfect Gurusevā. His perfect Gurusevā example will inspire us to follow Guru agnā (i.e., Śrῑ Guru’s advice or order) in letter and spirit, submit our hearts to Him, and fully engage our body and mind in His service (i.e., to implement His mission plans). By doing so, we will become true Gurudāsa. Being a true Gurudāsa is extremely important because, for a true Gurudāsa, Śrῑ Guru removes all kinds of worldly desires from his heart. The worldly desires, called anarthas, are barriers or obstacles on the way to pure bhakti. One most prominent anartha is pratiṣṭhāsā (i.e., desire for material establishment). Śrῑla Gurudeva has mentioned in Śrῑ Manaḥ-Śikṣā that pratiṣṭhāsā is the root or mother of all anarthas. As long as it remains in the heart, deceit will be there and pure bhakti will not come. Only a bonafide Guru can eradicate it from the disciple’s heart. It is for this reason that Śrῑla Gurudeva has told us many a time, “Give me your heart; I will give you Krṣṇa prema”. For our own spiritual benefits, we must follow His advice. In His numerous lectures Śrῑla Gurudeva has advised us to be happy with whatever is achievable with minimum efforts. He warned us not to hanker after anything that will require extensive involvement in material activities. Śrῑla Gurudeva gave this warning because if we keep working hard all the time for illusory material happiness, then we will have no time to carry out our most important duty, which is to render loving service to Śrῑ Śrῑ Rādhā Krṣṇa Nitya Yugal.

We are very fortunate to have Śrῑla Nārāyaṇa Gosvāmῑ Mahārāja, a talented Mahābhāgavata Vaiṣṇava, as our Gurudeva. As His disciples, our foremost job is to make Him happy through proper Gurusevā. Even though Śrῑla Gurudeva has disappeared from this world, we must keep serving Him whole heartedly, because our relationship with Him is eternal. Śrῑla Gurudeva has shown that true Gurusevā begins with the disciple’s total surrender to Śrῑ Guru. Therefore, we must give our heart to Śrῑla Gurudeva, engage our body and mind totally to implement His mission plans, and follow His āgnā at any cost. We must lead an austere life and be happy with whatever is achievable with minimum efforts. Also, we will accept only those things which are favorable to pure bhakti and reject all those things which are unfavorable to pure bhakti (ānukulasya saṅkalpaḥ, prātikulasya varjanam). Book distribution is an essential part of Gurusevā and we must continue to distribute Śrῑla Gurudeva’s books and His recorded lectures. Further, we must give generous charitable donations to support Śrῑla Gurudeva’s Bhakti Projects, such as construction of temples, reprinting of Śrῑla Gurudeva’s books for distribution, and cow protection. Those of us who have the financial capability to contribute more money, they must contribute more money. Otherwise, it will be a case of viṭṭa śāṭhya (i.e., wealth stealing or cheating). Śrῑla Gurudeva has mentioned numerous times that everything including, money, wealth, wife, husband, children, etc., are for Krṣṇa’s enjoyment (i.e., Krṣṇa bhogyā), not for our enjoyment. We must remember and seriously consider His words while making charitable contributions.

Śrῑla Gurudeva’s scholarly books and audio and video recordings of His lectures are invaluable spiritual assets left by Him for our use. They bear the testimony to Śrῑla Gurudeva’s extraordinary talent and unparalleled spiritual potency. They will keep providing spiritual guidance to us in His absence. For instance, they will provide us guidance on how to transcend to the mode of pure goodness and render devotional service in the mode of pure goodness, and help us to attain Krṣṇa prema. Also, they will teach us the conclusions of our śāstras, remove our doubts about any difficult-to-understand siddhāntas, and provide answers to all of our spiritual questions. Additionally, they will help us in communicating Śrῑla Gurudeva’s messages to other people and encourage new people to join Śrῑla Gurudeva’s movement. As more people join Śrῑla Gurudeva’s spiritual family (i.e., iṣṭa goṣṭhi), we will have more volunteers to preach Krṣṇa Consciousness. In this way, Krṣṇa Consciousness movement will keep propagating and Śrῑla Gurudeva, our Guru paramparā, and Śrῑ Caitanya Mahāprabhu and His Divine Associates will be very happy.

Finally, on this very special day of Śrῑla Gurudeva’s appearance in this world, I offer my millions of dandavat pranāms at His lotus feet, and humbly pray to Him to bestow His causeless mercy on us so that we can become true Gurudāsa (maner ei abhilās hai yena Gurudāsa). Also, I beg to Him to remove pratiṣṭhāsā and other anarthas from our hearts.

Śrῑla Gurudeva ki jaya!

Aspiring for service to Śrῑ Guru and Vaiṣṇavas.
