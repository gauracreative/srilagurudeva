Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Sundar Gopal Das

Australia, Brisbane

Dear most worshipable Śrīla Gurudeva,

I am offering my koti, koti dandavat pranams at your lotus feet.

It has been ten years since you have left from our physical vision, but I am so unfortunate that I am not feeling any separation or lacking in my life. Now I have been given an opportunity to offer sraddha-puspanjali at your lotus feet on the most auspicious occasion of the centennial of your appearance.

Even though a baddha-jiva is not capable to glorify a transcendental personality like you, still for my own purification I am writing something.

Your magnanimity

Your love and affection extended beyond your direct disciples – anyone who came to you for shelter immediately got your love and teachings without hesitation. When we hear the classes that you spoke to the seniors devotees of ISKCON – the love you have for them is clearly felt. It also showed how you revealed your heart to them, considering them to be senior devotees who have practiced Kṛṣṇa-consciousness for a long time and your eagerness for them to advance further in that path of rupanuga bhakti.

You mentioned in your morning walks that, just as Śrī Kṛṣṇa reciprocates according to one’s surrenders and devotion, a guru reciprocates depending on the disciple’s surrender. Similarly, whoever came to you with a desire to learn and advance, you nourished them according to their situation and always enthused them to advance further in bhakti.

Devotees who were involved in publication at the other Gaudiya Mathas mentioned how you provided support and any material whenever they requested them. Similarly, your books were put on the Internet and could be downloaded by anyone without any costs.

Taking Responsibility

In one of your classes, you mentioned that one who doesn’t take responsibility is like an animal. You yourself showed throughout your life the proper way to take responsibility, starting with the early years of maintaining Śrī Kesavaji Gaudiya Matha (including its residents, your godbrothers, and later the disciples of Śrīla Vamana Gosvāmī Maharaja) and then publishing granthas and magazines, engaging in local preaching, handling court cases, and more. Responsibilities increased as you assumed the role of accepting disciples. The size of the parikramas increased but you were never deterred – instead you became so pleased at seeing so many pilgrims glorifying the Lord and engaging in Śrī Kṛṣṇa sankirtana. You took responsibility of whoever came to you with a desire to advance in spiritual life.

In addition to accepting the responsibility of your ever-increasing society, you also took the responsibility of protecting the sampradaya against allegations through publishing “Prabandha Pancakam – Five Essential Essays,” challenging the babajis when you visited Radha-kunda (who used to say that the Gaudiya Matha doesn’t follow a proper guru-parampara nor does the Gaudiya Matha follow the six gosvamis), and protecting rupanuga-dhara through your publications like Ujjvala Nilamani, Śrī Rasa Pancadhyayi, Śrī Gita Govinda, etc. You mentioned that even though not many devotees are qualified to read some of the granthas, still something should be available in the rupanuga-dhara or devotees will read the many editions available in the market and be misled instead. You gave ample warnings that one has to tread these topics very carefully, as evidenced by including Śrīla Bhaktisiddanta Sarasvati Thakura Prabhupada’s foreword and your introduction to Śrī Gita Govinda.

You didn’t take this responsibility to your guru-maharaja and guru-parampara lightly. In 2002, you came to Sydney after Gaura-Purnima for a few days before flying to Hawaii for your publication seva. Rama-Navami was approaching, so the local devotees requested you to extend your stay by three or four extra days to observe Rama-Navami before flying to Hawaii. When you inquired from Brajanath Prabhu about what it meant for your schedule, he replied that about two weeks would be affected with traveling, setting up, etc. and that you will only have about four weeks available for dedicated publication seva – at which point you decided against changing the schedule.

In addition, you also wanted us to be responsible in our activities and gave instructions according to our situation or status in life. You were not happy when we used to take time off work to be with you when you spent two months in Australia in 2008 (prior to your trip to Australia, we had spent about eight days with you in Fiji). You wanted us to be present in the festivals but to go to work during the non-festival times. You even stopped us from taking a day off to assist your sevaks when you manifested an illness pastime and had to spend two or three days in the hospital.

In this way, you showed by your instructions and conduct that we need to be responsible in our lives and not act whimsically.

Your natural humility

We try to be humble in our activities especially when associating with senior devotees, but you displayed your natural humility in all circumstances.

Once you performed a pastime of having back pain during the Murwillumbah festival in 2005. The local devotees requested a local physiotherapist to make a house call. When he arrived on the first floor where you were staying, he was carrying a small medical bag. You indicated your displeasure to us that we didn’t greet the physiotherapist at the door and carry his bag for him. You then indicated to us to set up the massage bed (i.e. before the physiotherapist does it). We were amazed at how respectful you were to him and then after the check-up, you instructed us to carry the physiotherapist’s bag to his car.

Even though you had tens of thousands of disciples all over the world including doctors, lawyers, scientists, etc. and you received the “Yuga Acarya” award, still nothing affected you.

In his kirtan, Śrīla Bhaktivinoda Thakura expanded on the explanation of the “trnad api sunicena.…” sloka

jīvana-nirvāhe āne udvega nā dibe
para-upakāre nija-sukha pāsaribe (4)

hôile-o sarva-guṇe guṇī mahāśaya
pratiṣṭhāśā chāḓi’ karô amānī hṛdaya (5)

kṛṣṇa-adhiṣṭhāna sarva-jīve jāni’ sadā
karôbi sammāna sabe ādare sarvadā (6)
–From the song Śrī Kṛṣṇa-kīrtane Ĵadi by Śrīla Bhaktivinoda Ṭhākura

"In the course of leading your life, do not give anxiety to anyone else. For the benefit of others, forget your own comforts. Even if you are a great soul endowed with all good qualities, relinquish the desire for honor and make your heart humble. Knowing Śrī Kṛṣṇa to be situated within all living beings, always respect and show affection to all."

You are the embodiment of this sloka.

An important component of Mahaprabhu’s Mission

I am extremely unqualified in trying to express what you have given, and this will be explained in more detail by your senior disciples who have realised what you have given.

You explained how all the acaryas in the line of Caitanya Mahaprabhu came to give what Mahaprabhu came to give as explained in the sloka “anarpita-carim-carat.…” Under the direction and inspiration of Mahaprabhu, each acarya emphasised a special aspect of this mission. Being empowered by Sriman Mahaprabhu, you emphasised the goal of raganuga bhakti, especially rupanuga bhakti, and how to attain that goal.

By speaking about the acaryas during their respective tithis as well as by presenting their articles/harikatha in your magazines, you indicated that we should not neglect or ignore their instructions, as they are all beneficial for us in achieving our goal and in particular provide a foundation for properly understanding and applying your message and instructions.

However, it is sufficient to say that you are an important part of fulfilling Sriman Mahaprabhu’s desire. It is very rare for one in the modern era to obtain genuine greed to become a rupanuga if one doesn’t follow your teachings as delineated in your publications and harikatha – what to speak about attaining perfection as a rupanuga.

Thus you wanted all your publications and harikatha to be easily accessible and available to all. But it is my great misfortune that I was unable to take your conduct and teachings with gravity. My conduct is not befitting a devotee, let alone your disciple.

You often used to say, “I am always with you. Don’t fear. If you have any doubts, you can call me. I will remove all your anarthas." You even showed that these statements are true, but I never paid attention to your words, and root of the problem is that I was maintaining the conception that you are a mortal being.

Even though I didn’t possess deep faith in you and I was unwilling to give up material sense enjoyment, still you have continued to show your mercy even after you physical disappearance. You gave:

a) The association of your satirtha Śrīla Bhakti Vijnana Bharati Gosvāmī Maharaja who, by his conduct and teachings, allowed me to see the valuable instructions you placed in your harikatha/publications that I had completely missed previously.

b) The association of your one-pointed disciples, who have taken your conduct and teachings with great gravity and who are endeavoring to fulfil your mano-abhistam as their life and soul.

In one of his articles, Param Gurudeva Śrīla Bhakti Prajnana Kesava Gosvāmī Maharaja gave valuable instructions for someone like me. He states:

“By performing Śrī Vyāsa-pūjā, one simultaneously serves guru and Kṛṣṇa. To remain under the guidance of guru-dāsa, honourable servants of Śrī Guru, is the pinnacle of service to Śrī Guru. I therefore offer my sincere prostrated obeisances at the lotus feet of all of the servitors of Śrīla Gurudeva. O servitors of Śrī Guru, it is you who are the true worshippers of Vyāsa-pūjā. Please bestow your merciful glance upon me. Please do not neglect me upon seeing my worthlessness, but kindly engage me in dāsya, or servitude, through glorification of Śrī Guru and Gaurānga. This is my heartfelt prayer at your lotus feet.”

On Guru Purnima 2008, you mentioned in your harikatha that "merely placing a garland on the neck of Sri Guru or offering at his feet a few flowers and fruits, some money, sweets, or cloth does not mean that real worship of the guru has been performed. The real Vyasa-puja is to surrender oneself completely at the lotus feet of Srila Gurudeva and to fulfil his desires. This means that one should completely offer one’s independence at the lotus feet of Sri Guru in a mood of surrender: ‘O Srila Gurudeva, I am offering everything that belongs to me at your lotus feet.’”

I know that I am unable to really perform Vyasa-puja, so I am trying to be under the guidance of your one-pointed devotees. They are in reality performing your Vyasa-puja.

On this auspicious occasion or your appearance, I am praying that I can become qualified to become a true worshipper of your Vyasa-puja – not only once a year, but at every moment of my life.

Your unworthy servant,
Sundar Gopal Dasa
