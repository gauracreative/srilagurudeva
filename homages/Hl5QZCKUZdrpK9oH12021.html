Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Murali Manohar Dasa

Australia, Brisbane

om ajnana-timirandhasya jnananjana salakaya
caksur unmilitam yena tasmai sri guruve namah
namah om vishnu-padaya radhikayah priyatmane
sri-srimad-bhaktivedanta narayana goswami iti namine
vande ‘ham sri guru-varam sri rupanuga-pravaram
vraja-rasa-rasikam ca kripa-murti narayanam

Translation : (I offer my prayers of adoration to that exalted sri guru Sri Srimad Bhaktivedanta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja, who is the very form of compassion. He is the best among the followers of Sri Rupa Goswami and is perfectly adept in relishing ‘vraja-rasa’).

I most humbly offer unlimited “dandavat pranamas” at the lotus feet of my diksha guru, His Holiness Mahavisnu Goswami Maharaja, to Srila Bhaktivedanta Swami Maharaja, to my dearmost ‘siksha-guru’ His Divine Grace Srila Bhaktivedanta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja, to Srila Bhaktivedanta Vaman Gosvami Maharaja, to Srila Bhaktivedanta Trivikram Gosvami Maharaja, to Srila Vyasadeva, and to our entire Rupanuga Guru-varga.

It is an auspicious occasion of immense joy & jubilation to have the privilege to glorify my venerable ‘siksha-gurudeva’ His Divine Grace Srila Bhaktivedanta Narayan Gosvami Maharaja, on the most auspicious occasion of his Centennial celebration. Sri Gurudeva, our spiritual master, who like a generous prema-laden cloud poured without discrimination, a rain of nectarian teachings from the ocean of love of God, Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu – the combined manifestation of Srimati Radharani and Sri Krsna.

Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Saraswati Thakura has explained that “Rupanuga” means one who proudly identifies that we are servants of the topmost “Rupanuga” (follower of Srila Rupa Goswami) namely the ‘parampara’ Gurus. Those devotees who have taken shelter of Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu, and who have taken the shelter of “Madhurya-rasa” ( the mood of transcendental amour of Sri Sri Radha-Krsna and proudly maintain their self-conception that they are the servants of the followers of Srila Rupa Goswami are known as “Rupanuga” ). It is most befitting to say that Srila Gurudeva is also a “Rupanuga” in the highest sense to an amazing superlative degree, as exemplified by his pastimes in this material world.

Sri Gurudeva’s life history is immensely extraordinary and exemplary. Srila Gurudeva was born in a village named Tiwaripur, located near the bank of the sacred Ganges river in Bihar, India. He comes from a family of devotees. His father's name was Pandit Baleshwarnath Tiwari and his mother was called Lakshmi-devi. His birth name was Sriman Narayana, but his grandmother called him Bholanath, as a kind of nickname. He was a very religious young boy, and was deeply attached to the name of Rama. He was quite athletic as well, winning various awards. This got him easy admission into police academy; he eventually became a police officer.

The first devotee to really inspire him was Narottamananda Prabhu, a brahmacari disciple of Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Sarasvati Thakur. Narottamananda later became Srila B.K. Madhusudana Maharaja, whom Srila Gurudeva saw as his initial siksa guru.. Anyway, this was his first encounter with the Gaudiya Math, which at that time was a huge society of Gaudiya devotees. This was an institution to which he would eventually devote his life. Well, he left hearth and home and began a journey to Navadvipa, the place of Caitanya Mahaprabhu's pastimes. In Navadvipa, with great devotion and enthusiasm Sriman Narayana Tiwari served the lotus feet of his Gurudeva Srila Bhakti Prajnana Keshava Goswami Maharaja. Srila Bhakti Prajnana Kesava Goswami Maharaj was a lion-like acarya bestowing the suffering jivas with divine affection of ‘Sri nama imbued with prema’. Also, he was the manifestation of the receptacle of Sriman Mahaprabhu’s prema and foremost preacher of prema-bhakti as propagated by Srila Rupa Goswami. In 1947 on Gaura Purnima Srila Bhaktivedanta Narayana Maharaja was given both Harinama & Diksha initiations and was given the name Gaura Narayana Das. Also Srila Gurudeva had great intimacy with Srila Trivikrama Maharaja, Srila Vamana Goswami Maharaja and also with Srila Bhaktivedanta Swami Maharaja who was much senior in age and to whom he considered as his ‘siksha-guru’.

He also rendered personal service and support to Srila Swami Maharaja which is well known. In the year 1952, on Gaura Purnima Srila Gurudeva took sannyasa, the renounced order of life, from Srila Bhaktiprajnana Keshava Maharaj, his eternal guru. Since then he established a famous math in Mathura, and also in Vrindavan, Govardhan, Delhi, Navadvipa and overseas as well. He has translated many important Vaishnava works into Hindi, and is considered as one of the leading Gaudiya Vaishnava acharya in all of India. To understand the erudition, the deep esoteric knowledge of Sastras and the ability to explain lucidly complex “siddhantas”, Srila Gurudeva was unsurpassed by anyone on this planet.

When one studies carefully his lectures as well as question and answer sessions and other books then only one can get a glimpse of his vast understanding on the highly confidential subjects of Raganuga Bhakti & Madhurya Rasa. Srila Gurudeva has written about one hundred books on Bhakti sastras. Also his books in Hindi, namely ‘Sri Ujjavala-Nilamani”, “Madhurya-Kadambini, Srimad Bhagavatam & others are very esoteric & inspiring. Since November 1977, Srila Gurudeva has unwaveringly been carrying out this final request by Srila Swami Maharaja providing insightful guidance and loving shelter to all who came to him seeking guidance, and through the medium of his English books was giving his purifying association and divine realizations to sincere searchers of truth all over the globe. Such was the compassion & non-sectarian attitude of Srila Gurudeva. For more than half a century Srila Narayana Maharaja had demonstrated and exemplified the pure, unadulterated life of utter dedication and loving service to his Gurudeva, Srila Bhakti Prajnan Goswami Maharaj, to Sriman Mahaprabhu, and the Divine Couple, Yugala-kisora, Sri Sri Radha-Krsna. Srila Gurudeva continued to illuminate the path all over the world for those who wished to discover and dive deep into the ecstatic ocean of Radha-dasyam, service to the radiantly beautiful lotus feet of Sri Krsna's dearly beloved, Srimati Radhika.

Srila Maharaj had very affectionate relationship with Srila Bhaktivedanta Vamana Maharaj and Srila Bhaktivedanta Trivikrama Maharaj, but he also had a deep affection for all his Godbrothers, considering them to be extensions of his Gurudeva, and gave them appropriate respect. Srila Maharaj was above sectarian narrowness and was therefore dear to all. Maharaja had endeared all the Vrajavasis by his acts of compassion. He renovated many rundown kundas, places of worship within the 84 ‘kosa’ of Vrajamandal and performed many social welfare activities for the Vraja-vasis. Srila Maharaja always respected the ‘acaryas’ of other samapradayas- he never belittled them, rather he respected and honoured them. He would also, from time to time, call an assembly of scholars & followers of other sampradayas and offer respect and have discussion. In particular, every year he arranged an assembly to glorify Srila Rupa Goswami, which lasted for several days giving an opportunity to other speakers and he would conclude last day with his astounding nectarian glorification of Sri Rupa Goswami.

By the divine will of providence, about twenty years ago, we first met Srila Bhaktivedanta Narayana Goswami Maharaj at Sriman Prakash Prabhuji’s home in Brisbane. We are forever indebted to Sriman Prakash Prabhuji for inviting us to meet and have darshan and audience with Srila Maharaja. Some years later by the insistence of Sriman Jagannath Prabhu and my sister Krsna Lila Dasi we – (myself & my wife Hari Priya Devi Dasi) heard the explanation of the mantras from Srila Gurudeva in Murwillumbah for which also we are eternally grateful to both of them. We had accepted Srila Bhaktivedanta Narayan Gosvami Maharaja as our siksha-Gurudeva and he was so kind to us to shower his immense mercy by explaining these mantras to us, which projected us into higher stages of bhakti. As time passes on, I find that I have become increasingly aware of your presence in my life. Even after your departure from this planet I am nevertheless inspired by your immaculate character, instructions and superlative examples and activities. On the wonderful day of your disappearance, I find myself reminiscing about all the special memories of you that are stored in the deep chambers of my heart. We are deeply touched by the kind words of advice in Hindi which you gave to my family when you twice graced our humble abode. We cherish happy memories of your association each time you came on preaching mission in Murwillumbah and Brisbane.

Each time when Srila Maharaj came to Murwillumbah, there were more than 300 devotees from here and worldwide anxiously awaiting to hear the nectarine outpouring of highly esoteric Harikatha. The mere presence of Srila Gurudeva electrified the whole atmosphere there. It was a uniquely wonderful sight to behold. Hearing his Harikatha, the devotees gathered there were surcharged with new vigor and enthusiasm for Bhakti. Srila Gurudeva, we have seen time and again how well versed you are in the ‘shastras’, how convincing you can be and how deep your personal realizations of Lord Krsna, Srimati Radharani and Sriman Caitanya Mahaprabhu are. You exuded an aura of love and compassion, humility and with these qualities you have endeared everyone young or old.

As a humble servitor of the Sri Gaudiya Vedanta Samiti, Srila Narayana Maharaja began to organize the annual Kartika Vraja Mandala Parikrama, which he continued to carry on up to his final days. Srila Narayana Maharaja was requested by his Gurudeva, Sri Bhakti Prajnana Keshava Goswami Maharaja to build temples, ashrams, & translate the books of Srila Bhaktivinoda Thakur & Goswamis from Bengali into his native language, Hindi which he performed with full vigor & enthusiasm. Srila Gurudeva has produced so many great Acaryas who are constantly distributing the coveted wealth of Prema Bhakti as distributed by Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu, and Sri Rupa Raghunath. This is the greatest contribution of Srila Gurudeva.

With deep gratitude, adoration & resolute faith, let us all offer pushpanjali at the lotus feet of Srila Gurudeva, who is our well-wisher, humbly begging him to shower his abundant benedictions thereby giving us Guru Nistha and thus enabling us to cross this sea of nescience and give us the eligibility to enter into the realm of Prema-Bhakti, culminating to the highest stage namely “Radha-Dasyam” as propounded by Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu and distributed freely by the Six Goswamis of Vrindavan.

Aspiring for a particle of dust of the lotus feet of Sri Hari Guru & Vaisnavas

Vancha-kalpa-tarubhyas ca kripa-sindhubhya eva ca
patitanam pavanebhyo vaisnavabhyo namo namah

Your humble servant,

Murli Manohar Das
(Diksha disciple of His Holiness Mahavisnu Gosvami Maharaja)
