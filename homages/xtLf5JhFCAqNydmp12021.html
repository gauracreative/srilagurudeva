Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Vishnu Das

United States, Houston

oṁ ajñāna-timirāndhasya jñānāñjana-śalākayā
cakṣur unmīlitaṁ yena tasmai śrī-gurave namaḥ

My Dearmost Srila Gurudeva,

Please accept my millionfold prostrated obesiances at your divine lotus feet. I also offer my millionfold prostrated obesiances at the divine lotus feet of Sri Srimad A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada, by whose transcendental arrangement I was able to meet your Divine Grace. Srila Gurudeva, on this most auspicious day of the 100th anniversary of your transcendental appearance in this material world, I am making a humble attempt to offer my heartfelt pushpanjali at your lotus feet.

I am the most foolish and fallen of all, yet my arrogance and false pride is higher than the highest peak of the Himalayas; I am neither a poet or a writer nor do I have any understanding or appreciation of your unlimited, transcendental glories yet I am venturing to say something in your glorification. Though this is akin to the insignificant firefly trying to show light to the effulgent Sun, I pray and hope that out of your causeless mercy you will accept this offering that does not meet the standards of either the devotional or the literary writings.

Srila Gurudeva, you came in my life at a point when I was desperately looking for proper direction. I was a young and immature devotee but when reading and hearing about Gaudiya Vaishnava topics, it felt there was some gap in the understanding of jiva-tattva, Ksna-tattva, Radha-tattva, and Gaura-tattva. I felt that bhakti had to be something internal and not an external exhibition of grandeur and oratory.

Srila Gurudeva, though I was reading authentic translations of Srimad Bhagavad Gita, hearing discourses and even myself speaking on the Gita, I did not know what the real essence of Gita was. But in the very first visit to our home in 1991 in the first lecture that you gave on the evening of the first day you eloquently explained that the essence of Gita was ‘man-manā bhava mad-bhakto mad-yājī māṁ namaskuru’ (BG 18.65). You explained that real bhakti is when can always be internally absorbed in Krsna. And that absorption has to be like that of the newly-wed girl who had come to Nandagram and at the very first sight of Krsna she was so enchanted that instead of churning yogurt she started churning a pot full of sesame seeds.

Srila Gurudeva, I was reading Srimad Bhagavatam and listening to the discourses every morning. However, I did not know whose glorification Bhagavatam really was. On the very first morning of your first visit to our home in 1991 the very first question you answered was why the name of Srimati Radhika was not mentioned in any verse of the Bhagavatam. You clarified that in reality, Her name is in every verse and the purpose of Srimad Bhagavatam is to glorify the unparalleled, unalloyed, one-pointed devotion of Srimati Radhika to Krsna. Without Her, Krsna is nirviśeṣa (impersonal) brahma and without Her glories, Bhagavatam is empty.

Srila Gurudeva, I had read Sri Caitanya Caritamrita and learnt that Sriman Mahaprabhu was the most munificent incarnation because He freely distributed the Holy Name but the real purpose of Mahaprabhu’s advent as described right in the beginning of Sri Caitanya Caritamrita was not understood.

rādhā kṛṣṇa-praṇaya-vikṛtir hlādinī śaktir asmād
ekātmānāv api bhuvi purā deha-bhedaṁ gatau tau
caitanyākhyaṁ prakaṭam adhunā tad-dvayaṁ caikyam āptaṁ
rādhā-bhāva-dyuti-suvalitaṁ naumi kṛṣṇa-svarūpam
–.....Caitanya Caritamritam, Adi, 1.5

The loving affairs of Sri Radha and Krsna are transcendental manifestations of the Lord’s internal pleasure-giving potency. Although Radha and Krsna are one in Their identity, They separated Themselves eternally. Now these two transcendental identities have again united, in the form of Sri Krsna Caitanya. I bow down to Him, who has manifested Himself with the sentiment and complexion of Srimati Radharani although He is Krsna Himself.

śrī-rādhāyāḥ praṇaya-mahimā kīdṛśo vānayaivā-
svādyo yenādbhuta-madhurimā kīdṛśo vā madīyaḥ
saukhyaṁ cāsyā mad-anubhavataḥ kīdṛśaṁ veti lobhāt
tad-bhāvāḍhyaḥ samajani śacī-garbha-sindhau harīnduḥ
–.....Caitanya Caritamritam, Adi, 1.6

Desiring to understand the glory of Radharani’s love, the wonderful qualities in Him that She alone relishes through Her love, and the happiness She feels when She realizes the sweetness of His love, the Supreme Lord Hari, richly endowed with Her emotions, appeared from the womb of Srimati Saci-devi, as the moon appeared from the ocean.

During your very first visit to our home in 1991, you mercifully gave us a glimpse of the deep esoteric meanings of these verses and the specialty of what Mahaprabhu came to give, the unnat-ujjvala-rasa that is so rare and knowledge of which is made available in this world only once during the day of Brahma or 8.64 billion years. Without understanding the purpose of Mahaprabhu’s advent and His mood, one cannot really be a Gaudiya Vaishnava.

Srila Gurudeva, had you not appeared, the essence of Bhagavad Gita, the glories of Srimati Radhika described in Bhagavatam and specialty of Mahaprabhu’s advent enshrined in Sri Caitanya Caritamritam would not have been properly understood. Even though the previous acaryas had explained all this tattvas, in the current time this knowledge was misunderstood and was almost lost. There was a very real threat to the pure and real siddhanta of Gaudiya Vaishnavism getting corrupted and diminished. But with your lucid, yet powerful discourses devotees all over the world are now understanding and appreciating what real Gaudiya Vaishnavism is. The seed of bhakti that was sown in the heart of many was shriveling and dying but you nurtured it back to good health with your sweet Harikatha explaining the main siddhantas of Gaudiya Vaishnavishm. For this unparalleled contribution of yours, the world will be eternally indebted to you.

Srila Gurudeva, when I used to read the pastimes of Gaudiya Acharyas and other saintly personalities as well as read the qualities of a Guru described in various verses (such as one below), I used to wonder if I would ever meet a great saintly personality like this.

kripa-sindhuh susampurnah sarva‑sattvopakarakah
nihsprihah sarvatah siddhah sarva‑vidya‑visaradah
sarva‑samsaya‑samchetta nalaso gurur ahritah
–Hari-bhakti-vilasa (1.46)

Only one who is an ocean of mercy, is complete in all respects, has all spiritual qualities, is constantly engaged in the welfare of all living entities, is selfless, is perfect in all respects, is realized in transcendental knowledge (of bhakti-siddhanta), is able to remove all doubts of the disciples, is not lazy and is always engaged in the service of Sri Hari is qualified to be called a Guru.

I always prayed to have darshans and shelter of such an uttama-mahabhagavat Guru. I had your first darshans in 1986 during Swami Maharaja’s (Srila Prabhupada) disappearance day festival at Krsna Balaram temple in Vrindavan. Though I did not know who you were, just seeing you I felt an electric current in my body, my eyes swelled with tears as it seemed that an eternal associate of the Lord has descended in front of my eyes. I had never experienced anything like this in my life. It seemed the Lord had fulfilled my desire, but it was only after four long years that I finally got to meet you.

Srila Gurudeva, the twenty years spent in your physical presence were the golden years of my life. Every day I cry and pray to somehow get back that time as when you were physically present, I was so foolish that I did not understand how precious each moment of your association was. I was engrossed in my material pursuits and thus did not spend as much as time as I could have in your association and service. Now, on your Centennial Appearance Day, I reflect on your unlimited glories that cannot be described even by the thousand hooded Sesa Naga, what to talk of a dumb person like me.

Srila Gurudeva,
The dear disciple of Srila Bhakti Prajnan Kesava Gosvami Maharaja
And one of his three foremost beloved ones
Given the name Bhaktivedanta
For you were expert in all bhakti-siddhnata (sarva-vidya-visaradah)
He called you Bhakta-bandhav
As you were friendly (mitra) to all.

Your divine personality (divya-kalevar)
Tall and effulgent persona
Deep blue oceanic eyes (nila-caksuh)
that always behold the dark blue Lord
Enchanting smile and sweet words
Captivated the hearts of one and all (sarva-akarshak).

An illustrious preacher of true Gaudiya siddhanta (gaudiya-siddhanta pracharak)
You clarified many siddhantic misconceptions
Through your transcendental literature
And thousands of brilliant discourses.

Loved by all Braja-vasis (braja-jana-priya)
You are the most precious jewel of Braja (braja-ratna)
(So was said by a sannyasi at Radha Damodar temple)
They honored you as Acharya of the Millennium (Yuga-acharya)
For you helped restore many pastime places of Radha Krsna.

The foremost preceptor of Braja-rasa in current times
You taught the specialty of Braja-bhakti
You carried Braja in your heart
And only saw Braja everywhere.

Srimati Radhika’s beloved maidservant
You preached glories of Her love all over the world
Pleased by your unparalleled service
She sent a gift through the Seva-kunja priest.

Humbler than a blade of grass (trnad-api sunicena)
More tolerant than a tree (taror-api sahisnuna)
Never expecting any glorification (amani)
Giving immense respect and love to everyone (manadena)
You were always deeply absorbed in kirtan of Yugal Kishore (kirtaniya sada Hari).

Your genuine simplicity (saralata)
Your divine sweet voice (mrdu-vani)
Your soulful laughter
Your loving nature
Your mild demeanor (mrdu)
You non-duplicitous nature
Your friendly personal behavior (laukika-bandhuvat acarana)
Enchanted everyone who met you.

Always engaged in serving Sri Guru and Hari (satat-Hari-seva-nishtha)
Chanting, preaching, and writing
You never wasted a minute (avyartha-kalatvam)
And had no laziness (analasah).

Supremely magnanimous (vadanya)
You showered your mercy on anyone who came to you (kripalu)
Well-wisher of all (sarvopakarakah), and
Full of real compassion for all (karuna)
Equal to all (sama-darshi) yet unattached (nispriha)
You gave shelter to anyone who came to you.

Grave (gambhira) and silent (mauni)
You uttered no word other than Krsna-nama and Krsna-katha
Always peaceful (santa) and non-defiant (akrta-droha)
No situation ever disturbed you (sukha-dukhe-samam)
Free from all dualities (dvandvaih-vimuktah)
You were completely dependent on Sri Hari (Kṛṣṇaika-śaraṇa).

Completely self-controlled (vijita-ṣaḍ-guṇa)
You had absolute control on all urges
Truthful in your conduct (satya-sara), and
Truthful in your determination (satya-sankalpa)
Expert in all transcendental knowledge (sarva-vidya-visaradah)
You could remove every spiritual doubt (sarva-samsaya-samcchedah)
You were thus the Jagad-Guru
Qualified to make disciples all over the world.

You were the knower of everything (sarvajna)
Nothing could be hidden from you
For you were eternally liberated (nitya-siddha)
Beloved associate of the Supreme Lord (nitya-parikar).

Expert in every service (daksa)
You were an epitome of spiritual perfection
A great orator and poet (kavi)
You were the sweetest singer of bhajans (kirtaniya)
Completely faultless (nirdosa)
You were complete in all respects (susampurna)
And full of all qualities (sarva-guna-visista).

Srila Gurudeva, your qualities are unlimited
Your love is unlimited
Your compassion is unlimited
Your glories are unlimited
I only pray that I always stay absorbed
In unlimited meditation on you.

Srila Gurudeva, for engaging me in some menial service for the worldwide celebration of your divine 100th Appearance Day. Though I am completely unqualified, you somehow gave me the service to help plan and coordinate different initiatives. But due to the current world situation many in-person festivals are not happening at the scale we had planned for. However, devotees have come together virtually and conducting many online sessions to share your memories and glories. This has re-charged your sanga and devotees are now feeling very enthusiastic to work together to carry on your mission. I sincerely pray for your blessings that this mood may continue, and your teachings be spread with great love and harmony.

Srila Gurudeva, a team of very dedicate devotees worked for months to develop this Centennial Vyasa-puja website. Though there were unsurmountable challenges, by your mercy alone, this first ever Vyasa-puja website has manifested. I want to sincerely thank everyone who helped in this seva and hope that you will be pleased with this small offering at your lotus feet.

Srila Gurudeva, in our second meeting in early 1991 with your Divine Grace, you told us that you can teach us how to build a temple in our hearts where Sri Yugal Kishore, Sri Sri Radha-Krishna will come and reside. And for the next twenty years you painstakingly taught us how to build this man-mandir (Temple of heart). However, I am so unfortunate that despite you, a maha-bhagavata, eternal associate of the Lord, so mercifully giving this knowledge (of rupanuga-bhajan), due to my unlimited anarthas and lack of unconditional surrender, I have not been able to take even the first step in building this temple. But, Srila Gurudeva, I sincerely want to imbibe your teachings to purify the heart from all material desires and infatuations, and build a temple where I can welcome you and Sri Yugal Kishore and offer fragrant flowers of one-pointed loving devotion. So, on this most auspicious day of your Divine Appearance, I fall at your lotus feet, beseeching you to kindly forgive me for my unforgivable offences and spiritual digressions; accept this most fallen servant and give me the determination and qualification to follow your instructions to do rupanuga-bhajan and one day be able to build a beautiful man-mandir.

Aspiring for your eternal shelter and service,

Your most fallen and wretched son,
Vishnu Das
