Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Saci Devi Dasi

Germany, Mayapur Seva Ashram

All glories to Sri Srimad Bhaktivedanta Narayana Gosvami Maharaja!

My dear father,

I humbly bow down at your lotus feet and pray to be able to do so for the rest of my life and also after. I am also praying for the ability to glorify you sincerely without the desire to get appreciation for doing so.

I remember how much impact those words of yours had on me when you addressed me in your letters as 'My dear darling daughter'. Even more captivating was when once at the airport you looked very deep into my eyes and said 'I am your father and you all are my children'.

Even my own father could not impress me with all his presents and affection like you did. How can it be so?

The only explanation I have is that your words come from pure, selfless and causeless love, not considering my qualifications or disqualifications. You are interested only in helping me, guiding me and purifying me to be fit to enter the eternal world where you eternally reside. Therefore your words and actions are pure and powerful. You are also showing and teaching me by your own example how to go there. If you would give only knowledge, my desire to serve would surely vanish very soon. Like in the example of the dog who does not stay with a master who is merely putting tilaka or kanti-mala on him, but does not feed him or give him affection.

While writing this, I am now considering how to follow your exemplary character and instructions. How long will I stay in this world? What really matters?

You are continuously showing me that I‘m helpless and insignificant. My own ideas and plans have no stand on this path of devotion. I should only try to be your disciple and darling daughter in a true sense. I remember very well those moments when you looked at me very deeply and with gravity, right inside to my heart, cutting all my unwanted desires. The desire to endeavour for bhakti is your mercy alone.

How can I please you, my father and master?

It is seen even in this world that one can please the parents by showing love to their children. Your mercy and love also come through your devoted servants who understand your heart. They teach me how to serve you and how to understand your mood. You are never alone but eternally surrounded by them.

I have also heard that two things are important in your physical absence: manasi-seva and murti-seva. Before Krishna left for His eternal abode He spoke to Uddhava about it. He also told him about the importance of Srimad Bhagavatam, so that Uddhava could stay connected with the Lord.

I have the same instruments now, as well as good teachers in this school of love in separation. Therefore I’m praying today that you may give me the intelligence to be able to serve your sincere devoted disciples, so that you will be pleased.

Vaisnava dasi abhilasi,
Saci Devi Dasi
