Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Radhika Dasi

Canada, Toronto

Gurudev and Me
nama om visnu-padaya radhikayai-priyatmane
sri-srimad bhaktvedanta-narayana iti namine

When I was seven years old, Srila Gurudeva had entered the eternal pastimes of Sri Sri Radha-Krishna. When I was a baby, he held me in his lap and very mercifully fed me prasadam.

My mother tells me that once we were in the Govardhan Matha. I was crying loudly and nobody could make me quiet. My mother was worried since she saw Gurudeva walking towards her, and thought how she would get me to be quiet. However, I turned my head towards Gurudeva as he came near and stopped crying immediately and stared at him in awe with my jaw dropped. My mom says it was so magical. He was walking with Pujyapad Madhava Maharaja and I kept staring at him, dumbfounded. My mom told him, “Gurudeva, she was crying so loudly but when she saw you she stopped immediately!” Gurudeva smiled at me, and Madhava Maharaja said, “Issne apne guru ko pehchan liya hai,” (she has recognized her guru).

My parents tell me that one day we were in Gurudeva’s room and I was just three years old at that time. Gurudeva just looked at me and said “Ek kaala lardka hai Vrindavan mein, uski aankho mein mat dekhle naa” (There is a black boy in Vrindavan, do not look into his eyes). There were these sweet but limited interactions with Gurudeva, which my parents have told me about, but unfortunately, I do not remember meeting him at all.

My parents always tell me about how Srila Gurudeva was so benevolent to visit our house in Gurgaon and how lucky they were to serve him.

I always respected him because we have his pictures all over the house and my parents always shared their experiences with Gurudeva after his disappearance. However, I did not have a special place for him in my heart. By Gurudeva’s causeless mercy, sometimes I would sing kirtans in my home temple and read the meanings of those kirtans. In Sri Gurvastakam and other kirtans on Sri Guru, I read that without the mercy of Sri Guru, one cannot enter into the path of bhakti and one cannot advance in bhakti. By doing kirtans and doing arati with my mother, I gradually started realising how important Gurudeva really is for my parents and all the other devotees.

I started getting attracted to Gurudeva and started to watch his morning walks, lectures, listened to his sweet kirtans, read his books, and heard devotees glorify him. (There is a beautiful YouTube channel called “Remembering Srila Gurudeva” where devotees glorify him). When I watched his videos, I felt so close to him, as if I was sitting near him in his harikatha or walking with him in his morning walks. I had never felt so much bliss and happiness in my life as I felt by hearing the sweet voice of Gurudeva, his loving chastisement to devotees, his loving arguments with his godbrother Srila Trivikrama Gosvami Maharaja, his funny jokes during his lectures, and I loved sinking into his ocean-blue eyes in his pictures and videos.

There is a video on YouTube where Gurudeva was giving a lecture. He saw someone in the audience and in the middle of the lecture he said, “Come on.” He signalled with his hand to the devotee to come sit in the harikatha. “Oh, you should come on. Yes, come on, come on.” This was actually very cute and funny; when you see the video you will realize this (YouTube: "Srila Bhaktivedanta Narayana Maharaja - Badger, CA USA - 1998 - #1" - at 6:48 minutes).

Sometimes Gurudeva speaks in broken English intentionally so when devotees correct him, Gurudeva gives them a chance to serve him. This is just his lila (pastime). He is the most beautiful soul I have seen and will ever see.

Gurudeva explained that he had been associated with Rupa Gosvami all his life by keeping his instructions in his heart and following them. Gurudeva was a personification of Srila Rupa Gosvami’s instructions and thoughts. Therefore, it is possible to associate with Gurudeva too, even after his physical disappearance from this world. (However, he only set an example for us; Gurudeva is an eternal associate of Rupa Manjari.)

Anyway, despite the fact that I felt so close to Gurudeva when I watched his videos, I still felt so degraded because I did not remember meeting him and I had not taken diksa from him, so I thought that it did not make me his “official disciple.” However, Sundar Gopal Prabhu very mercifully told me that the harinama guru is actually the main guru because harinama will take you to Goloka-Vrndavana (Gurudeva had given me harinama in 2010). I was very relieved to hear this and I am so thankful to Sundar Gopal Prabhu.

Gurudeva also explained that once he goes to some place, he does not leave from there. I think if Gurudeva visits a disciple’s house or even if he enters someone’s heart, he will not go from there. Sometimes, we may divert our attention from Gurudeva, but that is only temporary. We are his responsibility, we are his sons or daughters, even if we go on a different path he will not let us go there, and will eventually take us back on the right track and keep us under his shelter.

There are so many people who have not met him but only heard about him and fell in love with Gurudeva. There was a girl (I won’t mention her name) who only heard about Srila Narayana Gosvami Maharaja about two days before my offering was completed, and she already wished that Gurudeva could have been her diksa guru! I realised by his mercy, that he has a relationship with so many people. Even if some people did not have so much affection for him, he cared for them unconditionally. Gurudeva has taken responsibility not only of his disciples, but the children of the children of his disciples. Gurudeva is a spiritual being; he is not a normal living being, and therefore there is no other way for us to remember him without his mercy. The people who have not met him are not remembering him by their own desire; Gurudeva is remembering them that is why they have kept Gurudeva in their heart. It is only because of the mercy of Srila Gurudeva. In spirituality, there is a two-sided relationship, not one-sided. If you are feeling sad or broken, your Gurudeva feels your pain. He is happy in your happiness and he is with you in your despair. It is because he loves you so much and he has such a benevolent nature to care for all the jivas (living beings).

I am certain that he is the one who very mercifully placed himself in my heart, not me. I hope that he will give me diksa (second initiation), maybe in my dream. Maybe he wants to save me from maya by giving me diksa, so he started a fire in my heart to receive diksa from him; otherwise, how else would this desire come in my mind?

Once, Gurudeva came in my dream and I touched his lotus feet. Then, I sat on the floor in front of his vyasasana (throne) with my back facing towards Gurudeva and he put his lotus feet on my shoulders to rest his feet! How merciful is he? Another day, I was talking to some devotees on a zoom chat about Gurudeva’s glories and during the time I was speaking, I was a hundred percent certain that I was stuttering and mumbling on my words and felt a little embarrassed in front of everyone. However, when I watched my recording I was so shocked to see that my speech was actually crisp and clear! This experience was almost “magical” because I clearly remember myself stuttering. However, it was actually Gurudeva speaking through me that is why there were no mistakes.
In another dream, I was feeling bad that Gurudeva was not physically present in this world anymore. Then, I opened the door of my room and from the balcony outside my room, I saw Gurudeva giving a lecture in my living room downstairs. I saw him from upstairs and froze. I was so happy because I actually saw my Gurudeva and I was not seeing him through a digital screen. I practically flew down the stairs in less than three seconds (it was only a dream) and did a dandavat pranam right in front of him, touched his feet and sat in the harikatha, staring in his eyes in tears. Then, he looked at me with his blue eyes and more tears streamed down my eyes, and the dream ended. I am so grateful to Gurudeva for this dream because I felt that I was actually meeting him for the first time. These are some ways he performs his sweet lilas (pastimes).

Gurudeva is walking with us on a path and the destination is Goloka-Vrndavana. He can never forget us; he is with us all the time because he is present in our happiness and downfalls of our lives. In the “Gurudev Kripa Karke” kirtan, there is a line “nahi naath bhulana mujhe iss jag mein ekela hun” (which means please do not forget me; I am alone in this world). Our family and friends are temporary but Gurudeva is the only one who is with YOU forever.

Srila Gurudev ki jaya! Gurudeva Centennial ki jaya!
