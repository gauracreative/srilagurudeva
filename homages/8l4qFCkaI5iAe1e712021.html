Srila Gurudeva Centennial 2021

Śrī Śrīmad Bhaktivedānta Nārāyana Gosvāmī Mahāraja  •  100th Anniversary

Paramananda das

United States, Gaitherburg Maryland

Srila Gurudeva ki Jai

Dear Srila Gurudeva,

Please accept my humble obeisances thrown in the dust of your lotus feet.
All glories to Sriman Mahaprabhu and Sri Sri Radha-Vinodbihari jiu.

In 1986 while serving in ISKCON Vrndavana, I was sent by the temple president Damodara Pandit Prabhu to Mathura to collect donations for the annual Janmastami souvenir printed by ISKCON Vrindavana. He said “I want you stay at Srila Narayana Maharaja’s temple in Mathura. It is a Gaudiya Matha.” I asked if that was okay to stay there. He replied “Srila Narayana Maharaja was very close to Srila Prabhupada and knew him for years before coming to USA and that is where Srila Prabhupada took sannyasa. Also Srila Prabhupada’s deity of Sri Mahaprabhu is there.”

I was surprised because I was always taught that Gaudiya Matha devotees do not like Srila Prabhupada and I was of course very wrongly indoctrinated in ISKCON about this.

Srila Gurudeva, you gave classes every morning and I would sit and listen even though I did not understand Hindi, so you kindly translated the classes for me personally. I especially recall one class about how Laksmi-devi could not enter the rasa-lila. You really got into the details, that I had never heard before that Laksmi-devi was still attached to her husband Lord Narayana. She was also attached to the opulence of Vaikuntha, and was not willing to become a simple gopi of Vraja, making cow patties with her lotus hands, thinking them too exalted for such a service. You went on and on and I had read this pastime in Caitanya Caritamrta when Lord Caitanya explained this to Venkata Bhatta.

After class you invited me into your room, Srila Gurudeva, and asked me many personal questions. You were more concerned about me than anyone I had ever met in ISKCON, I very quickly understood why you were a dear friend and god-nephew of Srila Prabhupada. You also had a set of Srila Prabhupada’s books in your small room. Soon many Mathura-vasis came and met you and took guidance from you. I was so impressed by your genuine love and concern for everyone, and I could tell very fast that you were free from all material desires. You had no ambition for name, fame, profit, and distinction. You were always chanting japa that I really liked very much, because in ISKCON one would often get told to not chant so much japa but I would see you chant constantly when you were not preaching to someone.

I was there in Mathura for around a week visiting all the life members of ISKCON in Mathura and ask for donation for the Janmastami souvenir and the shops had placed advertisements in that souvenir. After this, I would visit you every year when I was in Vrindavana and you would also always be happy to meet me again.

In 1989 you advised me to chant 64 rounds daily and you also told me that “I can tell you are very much attached to the priya-narma sakhas. I am sure this is your sthayi-bhava; your eternal relationship with Sri Sri Krsna Balarama.” You told me to daily study Manah-siksa by Srila Raghunatha das Gosvami and especially to read all the books of Srila Bhaktivinoda Thakura very carefully, and you pointed out Caitanya Siksamrta would be very important for me. I took these instructions to heart gradually. I had been chanting 64 rounds on and off since I joined ISKCON, but not steady everyday, year after year; that would take me time to get to that standard.

I shared your association with some devotees in Vrindavana and that is when the infamous gopi-bhava club started because many senior ISKCON devotees went to you and asked you about Krsna and the gopis. However, they did not take your instruction to chant 64 rounds daily to heart. So it was an artificial interest in the gopis that Srila Prabhupada did not like. He had banned a previous gopi-bhava club in the 1970s. Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Saraswati Gosvāmī Thakura Prabhupada has explained that it is okay to study all the books of the Gosvamis and one should be eager to read all of them but one should also be chanting 64 rounds daily and within 20 years one should assimilate all these teachings. Fingers were pointed at you, Srila Gurudeva, but you merely tried to help these devotees. Srila Prabhupada told you in 1977, if anyone from ISKCON, comes to you please help and guide them. So you always wanted to help us all.

You started your world tour in 1996. At that time I was serving in Bhaktivedanta Manor in England, distributing Srila Prabhupada’s books and you came to the temple. That day the temple president had asked me to lead Guru-puja. You told me my singing of the Guru-puja was very beautiful and powerful. I was honoured to hear this from your lotus mouth. I felt it even though I didn’t deserve it.

I went to see you in the house of Sridama Sakha Prabhu. I had a question it was related to your statement that Lord Nrsimha once appeared as half-cat and half-lion. You told me, Srila Gurudeva, that you read this in the commentaries of the acaryas and was not sure where, but you said “You would look into it.” Later I found out that this form is Bidal Nrsimha. I told you that I wanted to write a book about Lord Nrsimha and you asked me some questions. You said “Your research will be useful and this book will be very important for Gaudiya Vaisnavas.” I felt honoured you would say something like that I had not even written the book yet, but I took this as important encouragement. Because I did not feel myself qualified at all. But you always made us all feel qualified and that we could become more and more qualified and more and more empowered in Krsna consciousness. You fanned everyones’ sparks of bhakti and made them into fires and blazing fires.

In 1999 I took re-initiation from you and you gave me the name Paramananda das and told me to chant 64 rounds daily. In 2001, I was made manager for your festival in Virginia attended by more than 1000 devotees. It was a week-long harikatha program that was held in Prince Williams forest park. You were very pleased with me and asked me to take sannyasa and travel with you all over the world. I had entered grhastha-asrama and my son was only three years old, so I asked you “If you think I should still leave grhastha-asrama with such a small son?” You said “Stay in grhastha-asrama but be detached like Sukadeva Gosvami who walked out on his father Srila Vyasadeva at the time of his birth thus giving up all attachments to this world.”

There are so many beautiful things I can say about you Srila Gurudeva, but I want to make this offering short and to the point. I am missing your association and I pray that I may serve in Goloka Vrndavana under your guidance.

Your eternal servant,

Paramananda das
